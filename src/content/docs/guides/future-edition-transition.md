In January 2026, FIRST and LEGO Education announced a major change to how FLL works. This guide explains what's actually changing, what it means for your team today, and what decisions you need to make — without the press-release spin.

:::caution[Information freshness]
The Future Edition launches in August 2026. Details are still being released. Check [firstlegoleague.org](https://www.firstlegoleague.org) and your PDO for the latest — this guide reflects what was publicly announced as of May 2026.
:::

---

## The short version

- The current SPIKE Prime-based program (**Founders Edition**) continues through 2027/28 — two more full seasons.
- A new hardware platform (**Future Edition**) launches in August 2026 for the 2026/27 BIOGLOW season.
- **Both run simultaneously** for two seasons (2026/27 and 2027/28). Teams choose which to enter.
- From 2028/29 onward, only Future Edition remains.
- Future Edition hardware is **not backward-compatible with SPIKE Prime.**
- SPIKE Prime is **retiring June 30, 2026** from LEGO Education's product line — but existing sets remain usable for Founders Edition through 2027/28.
- LEGO Education's partnership with FIRST ends after 2026/27; LEGO Education plans to run a standalone 2027/28 season of their own.

---

## What is the Future Edition?

The Future Edition replaces SPIKE Prime with the **LEGO Education Computer Science & AI** platform — new hardware built around wireless connectivity, motorized task models, and a new programming environment.

Key gameplay changes in Future Edition:
- **Semi-cooperative matches** on a shared field (two teams work on the same table at the same time, with both competitive and collaborative elements)
- **Four defined team roles** (instead of each team doing everything the same way)
- **Wireless hardware** — no cables to the hub during matches
- **Motorized task models** — some missions involve models that move or change state

What stays the same:
- Three-component structure: Robot Game + Innovation Project + Core Values
- Age eligibility (9–16)
- Team sizes (2–10 kids)
- Season theme each year

## The two-track transition (2026/27 and 2027/28)

| Season | Founders Edition | Future Edition |
|---|---|---|
| 2025/26 (UNEARTHED) | ✅ Active — SPIKE Prime | ❌ Not yet |
| 2026/27 (BIOGLOW) | ✅ Still active | ✅ Launches |
| 2027/28 | ✅ Final season | ✅ Continues |
| 2028/29 onward | ❌ Ends | ✅ Only program |

During the two-track years, teams register for one track or the other. They compete in separate divisions at tournaments that support both.

## Should you buy SPIKE Prime right now?

**Buying a new SPIKE Prime set today (mid-2026)?**

SPIKE Prime officially retires from LEGO Education's product line on June 30, 2026. After that, new sets may still be available from retailers with leftover stock, but LEGO Education won't produce more.

- If you're planning to compete in **2025/26 (UNEARTHED)** — still in progress for most teams — SPIKE Prime is the only option. Buy or borrow one if you need it.
- If you're planning for **2026/27 (BIOGLOW):** you have a choice. Founders Edition = existing SPIKE Prime; Future Edition = new hardware. If your team is experienced and wants the new gameplay, wait for Future Edition hardware. If you want a smoother on-ramp, Founders Edition continues.
- If you have **existing SPIKE Prime equipment:** keep it. It remains fully usable for Founders Edition through 2027/28. No need to replace it immediately.

**Bottom line:** Don't rush to buy SPIKE Prime if you're not yet mid-season. Wait to see Future Edition hardware pricing and availability before deciding.

## What about the LEGO Education partnership ending?

This is the part that confused a lot of families. Two separate things are happening:

1. **FIRST + LEGO Education partnership ends after 2026/27.** The "FIRST LEGO League" brand goes away. FIRST continues its own program (Future Edition) from 2028/29; LEGO Education runs a separate standalone 2027/28 season.

2. **The program your kid competes in** (FLL Challenge) continues under FIRST as Future Edition. It's the same competition DNA — Robot Game, Innovation Project, Core Values — with new hardware and some new gameplay mechanics.

For most teams, the partnership ending is background noise. What matters: the competition your kid enters in 2026/27 and beyond is still run by FIRST, still has tournaments, still has World Championship.

## What PDOs are saying

Regional PDOs are still publishing their own guidance for 2026/27 rollout. Most are:
- Hosting informational events for coaches in spring/summer 2026
- Planning to run both Founders and Future Edition tracks at 2026/27 tournaments
- Not yet publishing Future Edition registration details (as of May 2026)

Check your PDO's website for local guidance. If you're in the U.S., use [FIRST's partner finder](https://www.firstinspires.org/find-local-support) to find your PDO.

## Decisions your team needs to make (2026/27 planning)

| Decision | When to decide | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Founders vs. Future Edition for 2026/27 | Before registration opens (typically May–June) | If team has SPIKE Prime gear and little time to learn new hardware — Founders. If experienced team curious about new gameplay — Future. |
| Whether to buy Future Edition hardware | After hardware pricing announced | Wait for official pricing and availability before purchasing |
| Whether to sell/keep SPIKE Prime | After 2027/28 season | Keep through 2027/28 if entering Founders; assess afterward |
