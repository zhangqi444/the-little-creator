The competition season ends. Most teams stop meeting. The teams that come back stronger are the ones that didn't.

Off-season isn't about grinding year-round — it's about doing a handful of things that compound over time: practicing skills that the competition season never gives you enough time for, exploring new ideas without score pressure, and keeping the team together so you don't start from scratch in August (or January).

This guide covers practical off-season options for all six programs.

## Why off-season matters

| If you stop entirely | If you do off-season work |
|---|---|
| Start next season re-learning basics | Start next season building on what worked |
| Lose members who drift away over summer | Keep culture alive; returning members onboard new ones |
| Robot knowledge walks out the door with graduating members | Knowledge is documented and transferable |
| No time to experiment — season pressure starts immediately | New ideas get tested safely before the clock starts |

The biggest gains are usually in **driver practice** (underinvested during season) and **programming** (teams improve faster when they are not racing a qualifier deadline).

## Cross-program off-season options

These apply to all six programs.

### 1. Off-season and invitational events

Almost every program has unofficial off-season competitions run by teams, regions, or event partners. These are lower-stakes, often cheaper, and excellent for trying new strategies.

| Program | Where to find them |
|---|---|
| FLL Challenge | Check your regional PDO website and Facebook groups; some PDOs run spring scrimmages |
| FTC | FTCScout scrimmage listings, FIRST Community Forums, regional social media; many League areas run spring leagues |
| FRC | FIRST off-season events list (FIRST website); Cheesy Poofs Chezy Champs, Ri3D community events, district off-seasons |
| VEX IQ / V5 / U | RobotEvents.com — filter by Invitational and Spring or Summer in your region |

Off-season events are ideal for trying a riskier autonomous, testing a new mechanism, or giving newer team members real match experience under lower pressure.

### 2. Driver practice

Season pressure means most teams go into qualifiers with far fewer practice matches than they need. The off-season is the right time to fix that.

- Run realistic timed practice matches weekly, even without a full team
- Introduce new drivers and let them accumulate repetitions before the next season starts
- Video practice runs and review footage — you will catch things you miss in the moment
- Test the robot at a different location if possible (lighting, floor surface, and temperature changes all affect performance)

For FTC, FRC, and VEX teams: driver practice off-season also lets you evaluate whether your current drive base suits your style before you design next year's robot around it.

### 3. Engineering notebook catch-up and reflection

Most teams end the season with a partially-filled notebook. Off-season is the time to:

- Reconstruct missing entries while the season is still fresh
- Write a season retrospective entry: what changed, what you would do differently, what you are proud of
- Photograph final robot configurations and mechanisms for the record
- Set up a better documentation habit for next season (templates, section headers, photo schedule)

FLL teams: see [Engineering Notebook Guide](/guides/notebook-guide/).
FRC, FTC, and VEX teams: judges in year two will ask what is different from last season — your notebook needs to answer that question with evidence.

### 4. Skill-building workshops

No competition deadline means you can focus on skills that get squeezed during season.

**Programming:**
- Work through a new tutorial (PID control, path planning, computer vision)
- Refactor messy season code into something you would actually want to come back to
- Add telemetry and data logging you kept meaning to add but never had time for

**Building:**
- Rebuild a mechanism from scratch using a different approach
- Test different motor and gear combinations without worrying about breaking competition hardware
- Learn a new tool or technique (3D printing, CAD, milling)

**Judging and presentations:**
- Practice presenting to an unfamiliar adult (teacher, family friend, local professional)
- Record and watch yourselves — most teams have never seen how they come across in a presentation

### 5. Outreach and community

Off-season outreach is lower pressure and more memorable than rushed in-season events.

- Demo your robot at a school fair, library event, or community festival
- Run a robotics workshop for younger kids — teaching reinforces what you know
- Connect with local STEM programs, maker spaces, or university engineering clubs
- FRC and FTC teams: multi-visit school partnerships started off-season count more toward Connect and Impact award criteria than single-event demos

### 6. Documentation and knowledge transfer

Before graduating members leave, capture what they know:

- Record short walkthrough videos of key mechanisms and code systems
- Write a state-of-the-robot document covering what each system does, known issues, and how it was tuned
- Update your drive team strategy notes and autonomous documentation
- Have outgoing members train incoming members before they leave, not after they are already gone

For multi-year teams, this is the highest-leverage off-season investment.

## Program-specific suggestions

### FLL Challenge

The FLL off-season runs roughly May through July (season reveal in August).

- **SPIKE Prime free-build.** Remove competition constraints and let kids build whatever they want. Creativity comes out when there is no score pressure, and most kids enjoy robotics more than adults expect.
- **Revisit missions.** Replay the current season mat without any pressure. Kids who felt like missions failed because of anxiety often surprise themselves when it is just for fun.
- **Innovation Project research extension.** If your team's IP topic interested them, keep exploring. This builds research habits that carry into the next season.
- **Prepare for the new season.** Watch the reveal together as a team event when the new season is announced in August.

### FTC

FTC off-season runs roughly April through September (Kickback Weekend game reveal in September).

- **Rebuild or iterate.** FTC teams that come into season two with a clean, well-understood chassis outperform teams that start from scratch every year.
- **Engineering Portfolio foundation.** Draft the opening sections of next year's portfolio now. Write a retrospective on this season's engineering decisions while they are still fresh.
- **Autonomous programming depth.** Road Runner, motion profiling, and computer vision (FTC-Vision, EasyOpenCV) all take time to learn properly. Off-season provides that time.
- **Scrimmages.** FTC scrimmages and off-season invitationals let new drivers develop without qualifier pressure.

### FRC

FRC off-season runs roughly May through December (Kickoff in January).

- **Off-season events.** FRC has a rich ecosystem of off-season competitions (Chezy Champs, NERD, regional off-seasons). Attending at least one builds match experience and connects you with the broader community.
- **Impact Award.** Write next year's Impact narrative now. The strongest Impact submissions take six to twelve months of work to document properly. Start the evidence log in June, not January.
- **Ri3D-style summer build.** Not just for watching — student teams can run their own Robot-in-3-Days-style summer build to prototype concepts before Kickoff.
- **Scouting systems.** Design and build your scouting app or spreadsheet off-season. Teams that dominate alliance selection built their systems months before the first event of the year.
- **New member onboarding.** Run a summer build program for incoming students before Kickoff. They contribute earlier and the season moves faster.

### VEX IQ (VIQRC)

VIQRC off-season typically runs April through September.

- **Free-build sessions.** Remove all competition constraints. Give kids a pile of pieces and a challenge they invented themselves. Collaborative free-build reinforces Gracious Professionalism naturally.
- **Autonomous Coding Skills.** ACS tends to be underpracticed during season. Off-season is the right time to improve it — ACS is a direct Worlds qualification path via Robot Skills Champion.
- **Teach younger students.** Older VIQRC students teaching younger ones deepens their own understanding and builds community.
- **Summer camps.** Many VEX Education partners and RECF Event Partners run summer camps. Attending or volunteering builds skills and connections.

### VEX V5 / VRC

VRC off-season is brief (April to May) with the new game releasing in May for the following season.

- **Analyse the new game immediately.** VRC reveals in May. Teams that spend May through July dissecting game design, scoring, and strategy come into September with months of advantage.
- **Prototype early.** Build simplified prototypes of key game mechanisms before committing to a full design. Off-season iteration is free; in-season iteration is expensive.
- **Skills Challenge focus.** Skills scores count toward Worlds qualification. Running Skills at off-season invitationals builds toward the Robot Skills Champion path.
- **Notebook foundation.** Teams with strong notebooks start writing in May at the new game reveal, not October. The engineering process starts at the design stage, not when you are already building.

### VEX U (VURC)

VURC runs with roughly the same May reveal and November through December start as V5RC.

- **New member recruitment and onboarding.** College membership turns over every year. Off-season is when you build the next cohort — workshops, interest meetings, structured intro builds — before season pressure begins.
- **PROS and LemLib depth.** Library-level autonomous programming (LemLib odometry, motion profiling, RTOS multi-tasking) takes significant learning investment. Off-season is when senior members can transfer this knowledge before they graduate.
- **Two-robot system design.** VURC's two-robot format is strategically complex. Off-season is the right time to reflect on role assignment, cross-robot coordination, and whether hardware choices served your strategy.
- **Signature Events.** VEX Signature Events run year-round. Attending an early-season Signature Event in October or November provides match experience and Worlds qualification points earlier than most teams start competing.
- **Academic integration.** Some teams integrate robot work into coursework (controls, computer science, mechanical engineering). Off-season is the best time to structure those connections with faculty advisors.

## What not to do

| Trap | Why it backfires |
|---|---|
| "We will take all summer off then start fresh" | Teams that fully stop usually lose members, forget what they learned, and spend the first month re-learning basics instead of building on them |
| Mandatory off-season meetings | Forced attendance in low-stakes time kills motivation; keep off-season optional but worthwhile and attendance self-selects for engaged members |
| Rebuilding everything before the new season is revealed | Wait for the reveal before committing to a new robot design — last year's frame and drive base may be perfectly serviceable under new game rules |
| Neglecting outgoing members | Graduating members who do not feel appreciated do not mentor the next cohort; a simple end-of-season celebration costs almost nothing |
| Off-season becoming work | If it feels like homework, kids will not show up; the best off-season activities are genuinely fun or interesting to the specific kids on your team |

## A minimal off-season checklist

If you do nothing else, do these three things before next season starts:

1. **Hold one end-of-season debrief.** Ask every team member: what worked, what did not, what would you change? Write it down. You will use it.
2. **Document the robot.** Photograph it, write down what you know about it, record a short walkthrough video. Thirty minutes now saves hours next year.
3. **Schedule your first meeting of next season before summer ends.** A date on the calendar keeps the team from fully dissolving. Announce the new season reveal as a team event.

Everything else in this guide is a bonus on top of those three.
