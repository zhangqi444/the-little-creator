Every competitive robotics season has the same failure mode: a team does excellent technical work, then misses a registration window, fails to request an event assignment, or arrives at a qualifier without the right paperwork. This guide maps the calendar for all six programs and gives you the checklist habit that keeps those things from happening.

:::note[Companion guides]
This is a logistics and calendar guide, not a season-strategy or judging-prep guide. For strategy, see [Planning Your FLL Season](/guides/season-planning/) (FLL) or the first-four-weeks guides for your program. For tournament day itself, see [Tournament Day Checklist](/guides/tournament-day-checklist/).
:::

| Program | Season starts | First events | Championship level | Registration window |

| **FLL Discover** | August | Any time (flexible, school-based) | No competitive events; celebration-focused | Flexible |

## FLL Challenge — Season Calendar

### Key dates

- **August** — Season reveal. Game and Innovation Project theme announced. Season kit (mission model set + season materials) ships to teams.
- **August–September** — Register with your regional PDO (Program Delivery Partner) if not already registered. Most PDOs open event registration shortly after the reveal.
- **September–October** — Build, program, and Innovation Project research.
- **November–December** — Qualifying tournaments. Most teams compete once; some PDOs allow multiple qualifiers.
- **January–February** — State / regional championship (invitation only — advance from qualifier).
- **March–April** — World Festival (Houston or equivalent; invitation only).

### What to register for and when

1. **FIRST national registration** — done once per season; creates your team number. Deadline is flexible but close it before event registration opens in your region. Cost: $225 (US).
2. **Regional PDO registration** — many PDOs have a separate fee and event assignment system. Some use FIRST Event Registration, others use their own portal. Check your PDO's page (see [Registration Reference](/guides/registration-reference/)).
3. **Event assignment** — in most regions, teams submit event preferences and PDOs assign them. Popular qualifiers fill fast. Don't wait.

### Common mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Registering nationally but not regionally | They are separate systems. Confirm with your PDO that they have your team on file. |
| Assuming last year's event registration carries over | It doesn't. Re-register each season. |
| Registering too late and getting an inconvenient assignment | Submit preferences immediately when the window opens. |
| Missing the Innovation Project submission deadline | Some regions require digital submission before the event. Check your PDO's specific requirements. |

## FTC — Season Calendar

### Key dates

- **July–August** — New team registration opens. Existing teams renew. Register early to secure your team number.
- **September** — Kickback Weekend: game reveal + engineering materials released. Season officially starts.
- **September–October** — Scrimmages and informal events in many regions. Not scored; good early practice.
- **October–November** — Official qualifier events begin in some regions.
- **December–January** — Peak qualifier season in most regions.
- **February–March** — State / Regional Championship (invitation from qualifier ranking or wild card).
- **April** — FTC World Championship (Houston).

### What to register for and when

1. **FIRST team registration** at ftc.firstinspires.org — opens July, costs $295 (US).
2. **State / regional program registration** — most states have a separate registration through their League or Program Delivery Partner. Some require this before event registration opens.
3. **League or qualifier event registration** — FTC uses a League structure in many regions. Joining a League means guaranteed matches across several dates rather than a single-day qualifier. Check which structure your region uses.
4. **Engineering Portfolio upload** — many regions require submitting your Engineering Portfolio (PDF) before or at the event. Don't wait until tournament week.

### FTC League vs. standalone qualifier

Many FTC regions run a **League model**: teams join a league that holds 3–5 meet events across the season before advancing to a League Tournament. Scores accumulate. This is different from FLL's single-day qualifier model. Know your region's format at the start of the season — it affects when you start competing and how often you need to be ready.

## FRC — Season Calendar

### Key dates

- **Late summer (August–September)** — Team registration opens for the upcoming season. Existing teams should renew early.
- **October–November** — Event registration opens. FRC uses a **district model** in many regions (teams accumulate points across district events) and a **regional model** in others. Understand your region's structure.
- **First Saturday in January** — Kickoff. Game revealed. Six-week build season begins.
- **March–April** — District and regional events. FRC is typically a 3-day event (Thursday practice, Friday/Saturday competition).
- **April–May** — FRC Championship (usually two events: Houston and Detroit). Invitation via district championship advancement or wildcard.

### District vs. Regional model

| Model | How it works | Advancement path |
|---|---|---|
| **District** | Teams compete at 2+ district events; points accumulate | Top teams advance to District Championship → Championship |
| **Regional** | One large event per region; teams choose which regional(s) to attend | Rank at regional → Championship invite |

Check FIRST's event search at firstinspires.org to see which model applies to your region. New teams often don't know which model they're in and miss the two-event requirement of the district model.

### Common FRC mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Missing the event registration window (fills fast in popular regions) | Set a reminder for when registration opens (usually October); register within the first week. |
| Not understanding district point requirements | District teams must attend 2 events minimum. Plan both at the start of the season. |
| Impact Award submission deadline missed | The Impact essay, video, and executive summary have separate deadlines before the event. See [FRC Impact Award Submission](/guides/frc-impact-submission/). |
| Arriving without robot inspection documentation | FRC requires robot configuration checklists and a specific inspection process. Review requirements at firstinspires.org. |

## VEX IQ (VIQRC) — Season Calendar

### Key dates

- **May** — Season reveal. New game announced. Teams can start building immediately if they have last season's kit (VEX IQ parts carry forward).
- **June–August** — Pre-season practice, hardware ordering, team formation.
- **September–October** — Team registration on RobotEvents. Event registration also through RobotEvents.
- **October–November** — First qualifier events.
- **November–February** — Peak qualifier season.
- **February–March** — State / Regional Championship (top teams by ranking or explicit advancement).
- **April** — VEX Worlds (Dallas). Invitation by state/regional advancement, Skills-only rankings, or Excellence Award pathway.

### What to register for and when

RobotEvents (robotevents.com) is the single platform for all VEX registration:
1. **Create an organisation account** if you don't have one.
2. **Register your team** — you choose a team number (VIQ teams: 4–5 digits + optional letter; confirm with your region).
3. **Register for events** through the event listings on RobotEvents. Events have limited capacity and open registration at different times. Check event pages regularly.

### VEX IQ Skills qualification path

VIQRC includes a **Skills-only path to Worlds**: teams with high Robot Skills scores (Autonomous + Driver) may qualify independently of match performance. Track your Skills scores on RobotEvents. This is especially valuable for small teams or teams who don't advance through state Championship rankings.

## VEX V5 / VRC — Season Calendar

VRC follows the same seasonal structure as VIQRC (May reveal, RobotEvents registration, October–April events) but with a higher competitive intensity and some structural differences:

| Aspect | VIQRC | V5RC |
|---|---|---|
| Season reveal | May | May |
| First events | October–November | October–November |
| Regional/State Championship | February–March | February–March |
| Worlds | April (Dallas) | April (Dallas, separate division) |
| Skills path to Worlds | Yes | Yes |
| Alliance match format | Collaborative (no opposing alliance) | Competitive (2v2 alliance matches) |
| Engineering Notebook required for awards | Yes | Yes, more weight at higher levels |

### VRC-specific calendar notes

- **Scrimmages** — Many VRC regions run early-season scrimmages (October) before the first official event. These don't count toward rankings but are valuable for calibrating your team's readiness.
- **Skills-only ranking** — Like VIQRC, VRC has a separate World qualification path through Robot Skills scores. Monitor RobotEvents Skills Rankings.
- **Autonomous Win Point (AWP)** — VRC includes an Autonomous Bonus/Win Point mechanic that affects standings. Plan this from week one of your season, not two weeks before the event.

## VEX U / VURC — Season Calendar

VURC follows the same May reveal and RobotEvents structure, but with university schedules creating different practical pressures:

- **May** — Season reveal. Teams often don't begin serious work until September when the academic year resumes.
- **September–October** — Team formation, registration, hardware ordering.
- **October–December** — Practice events and early qualifiers. VURC events are less numerous than V5RC; plan travel early.
- **January–February** — Regional / Signature Events. VEX Signature Events are open-invitation, high-prestige events that offer Worlds qualification. Worth targeting early.
- **April** — VEX Worlds (Dallas).

### VURC-specific calendar notes

- **Fewer events, higher travel requirement** — VURC has significantly fewer local events than V5RC or VIQRC. Teams often travel regionally or nationally for enough event experience. Budget and travel planning must start in September.
- **Signature Events as qualification path** — Signature Events are open to any registered team and offer Worlds qualification. Many VURC teams target one Signature Event per season as their primary qualification route.
- **Two-robot complexity** — building two competition robots doubles your logistics. Start ordering hardware in August; parts lead times compound semester schedules badly if you wait.

## Cross-program logistics checklist

These apply regardless of which program you're in.

### Before the season starts
- [ ] Renew or create your team registration on the appropriate platform
- [ ] Confirm your region's program delivery partner or league structure
- [ ] Note when event registration opens in your region (set a calendar reminder)
- [ ] Order hardware — lead times are real; act before the season reveal if possible
- [ ] Confirm your team's roster meets age/grade eligibility for your program

### At the season reveal
- [ ] Read the full game manual before making design decisions
- [ ] Note any submission deadlines (Impact essay, Engineering Portfolio upload, etc.)
- [ ] Register for events within the first week of registration opening
- [ ] Identify your target qualifier + backup qualifier (or league dates)

### 4–6 weeks before your first event
- [ ] Confirm event registration is active and your team is on the roster
- [ ] Review what documentation you need to bring (inspection checklist, notebook, portfolio, etc.)
- [ ] Run a practice session in match format
- [ ] Review judging requirements for awards your team is targeting

### Week of the event
- [ ] Re-read the event-specific rules (venue, pits, schedule, check-in time)
- [ ] Confirm travel and accommodation if needed
- [ ] Pack list from [Tournament Day Checklist](/guides/tournament-day-checklist/)
- [ ] Have printed or digital copies of: robot inspection form, engineering notebook/portfolio, team roster

## Managing multiple registration systems

| Program | Primary registration | Event registration |
|---|---|---|
| FLL | firstinspires.org | Regional PDO (varies) |
| FTC | ftc.firstinspires.org | FIRST Event Registration or regional system |
| FRC | firstinspires.org | FIRST Event Registration |
| VEX IQ | robotevents.com | robotevents.com |
| VEX V5 | robotevents.com | robotevents.com |
| VEX U | robotevents.com | robotevents.com |

One practical tip: **use a shared team calendar** (Google Calendar, Notion, or a printed sheet) to list every registration deadline, event date, and submission deadline at the start of the season. Update it when the game manual drops. This single habit prevents almost every logistical failure.
