## Official sources (canonical)

The official RECF landing page for VEX U. Covers eligibility (enrolled university students; faculty/staff sponsor required), the two-robot-per-team format, the season timeline, and links to the game manual and VURC knowledge base. First stop for any new team exploring VEX U.

The official RECF knowledge base specifically for VURC. Separate from the V5RC KB and VIQRC KB. Contains team registration how-tos, eligibility FAQ, robot construction rules (what custom machining and electronics are allowed), and the official Rules Q&A. Staff answers in the Rules Q&A section carry the same authority as the game manual — critical for disputing a ruling at an event.

- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** teams (university), coaches

VEX U plays the same game as VEX Robotics Competition (V5RC) — the game manual is the same document. **VURC-specific rules and build allowances are published as an addendum.** Download the main game manual + addendum from this page. Read both — the V5RC base manual covers the majority of the rules; the addendum covers what's different for university teams.

A free account is required to register; faculty advisors typically manage the official team record.

- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** teams, coaches, faculty advisors

VEX U uses V5 hardware as its base platform. This page covers the V5 Brain (full-color touchscreen, built-in wi-fi, built-in IMU), V5 Smart Motors with integrated encoders, and the full sensor/controller lineup. VEX U teams extend this with custom-machined structural parts and, in some categories, additional sensors — the VURC addendum specifies the allowed extensions.

## REC Foundation resources

VEX U competitions are operated by the **Robotics Education & Competition Foundation (RECF)**, a separate non-profit from VEX Robotics (the hardware maker). RECF resources cover grants, scholarships, training, and governance across all VEX programs.

### REC Foundation — program operator

- **URL:** https://recf.org
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** university students, coaches, faculty advisors, event partners
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** recf, vex-u, vurc, governance, official
- **Use when:** Understanding who governs VEX U, accessing cross-program policies, or looking for RECF-specific resources (grants, scholarships, mentor training).

RECF runs all VEX competition programs — VIQRC (IQ), V5RC (V5), VURC (University), and VAIRC (AI). RECF handles team registration policy, event partner accreditation, rules processes, mentor training, and the World Championship. For VEX U teams, RECF is the single governance body — important to know when submitting rules questions or appealing tournament decisions.

### RECF Scholarships — university student awards

- **URL:** https://recf.org/teams/for-participants/scholarships/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** university students competing in VEX U
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** recf, vex-u, scholarships, university, funding
- **Use when:** You're a VEX U student looking for scholarship funding. RECF and partner organisations offer merit-based scholarships to eligible VEX participants; graduate students may face eligibility restrictions — check individual terms.

RECF and partner universities have awarded over $110M in scholarships across VEX programs. VEX U students competing at qualifying events and the World Championship are eligible to apply for various partner scholarships. Check the linked page for current offerings and eligibility — some are region-specific or available only to students attending partner universities.

### RECF Trainings — coach and event partner courses

- **URL:** https://recf.org/recf-trainings/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** faculty advisors, event partners, new team leaders
- **Level:** beginner, intermediate
- **Tags:** recf, coach-training, professional-development, vex-u
- **Use when:** A faculty advisor or new team leader wants structured training on running a VEX U team, volunteering at events, or understanding the RECF judging framework.

Free RECF training tracks for adults supporting VEX teams. The faculty advisor track explains how the season and event structure works — registration, robot inspection, judging criteria. Event partner training is required to host an official tournament. Worth completing before attending your first event as a team leader.

### VEX Robotics World Championship — VEX U Division

- **URL:** https://recf.org/vex-robotics-world-championship/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** qualifying teams, students planning long-term
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** vex-u, vurc, worlds, world-championship, qualification
- **Use when:** Your team is approaching World Championship qualification, or you want to understand the top tier of VEX U competition.

All VEX programs culminate at the VEX Robotics World Championship, held in late April each year. VEX U has its own division. Qualification routes include: (1) winning or placing well at regional tournaments, (2) Robot Skills Challenge rankings on RobotEvents, and (3) Excellence Award at qualifying events. Check the [VURC KB](https://vurc-kb.recf.org/hc/en-us) for your region's specific qualification pathway.

## Programming resources

VEX U teams compete at a level where off-the-shelf VEXcode is usually not enough. Most competitive VURC teams use PROS or OkapiLib for advanced autonomous and motion control.

### VEXcode Pro V5 — official programming environment

- **URL:** https://www.vex.com/vexcode
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** teams (entry-level), coaches
- **Level:** beginner, intermediate
- **Tags:** vex-u, vex-v5, vexcode, blocks, c-plus-plus, python, programming, download
- **Use when:** Your team is just starting out, or for rapid prototyping and testing before porting to PROS. Also useful for students familiar with VEXcode from V5RC who need a quick on-ramp.

Official free programming environment for V5 hardware. Supports Blocks, Python, and C++. VEX U teams typically start here and migrate to PROS for competition — VEXcode C++ and PROS C++ share the same V5 API surface, making the migration manageable.

### PROS — open-source C++ RTOS framework for V5

- **URL:** https://pros.cs.purdue.edu
- **Authority:** independent
- **Audience:** university students with C++ or embedded-systems experience
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** vex-u, vex-v5, pros, c-plus-plus, autonomous, rtos, programming, open-source
- **Use when:** Your team needs multi-task scheduling, high-frequency sensor loops, integration with autonomous motion libraries, or a full development environment with proper debugging support.

PROS (Purdue Robotics Operating System) is the open-source alternative to VEXcode, built on FreeRTOS and targeting competitive V5 / VEX U teams. It supports real task scheduling, better serial debugging, and integration with community libraries. **Most competitive VEX U teams use PROS.** A basic C++ background (classes, pointers, compilation) is a prerequisite. The PROS documentation includes a migration guide from VEXcode C++.

### OkapiLib — motion control library for PROS

- **URL:** https://okapilib.github.io/OkapiLib/
- **Authority:** community
- **Audience:** university teams using PROS
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** vex-u, pros, okapilib, autonomous, pid, motion, odometry
- **Use when:** You need a well-tested autonomous motion control library on top of PROS — including odometry pose tracking, PID heading correction, motor abstraction, and async motion controllers.

OkapiLib is a mature autonomous motion library built for PROS-based VEX teams. Widely used at the VEX U and V5RC World Championship level. Provides `ChassisController` abstractions, odometry, PID tuning, and async motion commands. Pairs with PROS for complex autonomous routines. See also LemLib (below) as a more recent alternative.

### LemLib — autonomous motion library for PROS

- **URL:** https://lemlib.readthedocs.io
- **Authority:** community
- **Audience:** university teams using PROS
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** vex-u, vex-v5, pros, lemlib, autonomous, odometry, pid, motion, path-following
- **Use when:** You want a more current PROS-based library with odometry, heading correction, pure-pursuit path following, and an active maintenance community.

LemLib is the most widely adopted autonomous movement library in competitive VRC and VEX U (PROS-based). Supports wheel-odometry pose tracking, motion profiling, and heading correction. More actively maintained than OkapiLib as of 2025. Has a documented tuning guide and worked examples. PROS + C++ experience required.

### VEX Python API reference (V5)

- **URL:** https://api.vex.com
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** teams using VEXcode Python
- **Level:** intermediate, advanced
- **Tags:** vex-u, vex-v5, python, api, reference, programming
- **Use when:** Looking up a specific V5 Python class, method, or parameter — motor control, sensor readings, IMU, controller input.

The official VEX Python API documentation for V5. Covers all Python classes with method signatures, parameter descriptions, and return types. Most VEX U teams eventually move to PROS/C++, but VEXcode Python remains a valid choice for simpler tasks or for teams with more Python than C++ experience.

## Season and events

### RECF Signature Events

- **URL:** https://recf.org/teams/competition/recf-signature-events/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** competitive VEX U teams
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** recf, vex-u, signature-events, regional, championship
- **Use when:** Your team is ready to test beyond local events. Signature Events draw teams from multiple regions and serve as a major benchmark before the World Championship.

RECF-designated regional tournaments with broader team attendance than typical local qualifiers. For VEX U, Signature Events are often the first venue to encounter top-tier competition from outside your region. They may offer additional World Championship qualification pathways depending on current RECF policy.

### RECF Event Partners — finding your regional network

- **URL:** https://recf.org/event-partners/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, faculty advisors wanting to host or find events
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Tags:** recf, vex-u, event-partners, regional
- **Use when:** Identifying the regional RECF Event Partner who organises VEX U qualifying events in your area, or investigating requirements to host a tournament at your university.

RECF Event Partners organise local and regional qualifying events. For VEX U, many qualifiers are held at universities — sometimes by the teams themselves as a service to the community. The directory helps you identify who to contact for event schedules, judging, and co-hosting opportunities.
