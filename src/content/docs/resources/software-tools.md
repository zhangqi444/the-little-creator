A curated list of the software and tools FLL Challenge teams actually use. Like our other resource pages, every entry includes who it's for and when to use it. Page is FLL-primary; VEX tools are listed in a secondary section.

## Programming environments — FLL Challenge (Founders Edition)

### LEGO SPIKE App

- **URL:** https://education.lego.com/en-us/downloads/spike-app/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, teachers, families
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** fll, spike-prime, official, programming, block-based, python
- **Use when:** You are programming a Founders Edition SPIKE Prime FLL Challenge robot — the official tool, required for competition through the 2027/28 season.

Official LEGO Education programming environment for SPIKE Prime. Block-based for beginners; Python (MicroPython) for more advanced teams. Runs on Windows, Mac, iPad, and Chromebook. The authoritative tool for current FLL Challenge programming.

### Pybricks (independent SPIKE firmware)

- **URL:** https://pybricks.com
- **Authority:** independent
- **Audience:** teens, coaches
- **Level:** intermediate, advanced
- **Tags:** fll, spike-prime, python, alternative-firmware, free
- **Use when:** A team wants more capable Python on SPIKE Prime — multitasking, faster iteration, real Python (not MicroPython) — and is comfortable replacing the official firmware.

Open-source alternative firmware for LEGO SPIKE Prime, Mindstorms EV3, and BOOST. Replaces official firmware to enable more capable Python. Widely adopted by advanced FLL teams. Switching firmware is reversible, but may not be permitted in some regional events — check with your regional partner before competition day.

## Programming environments — VEX (secondary)

VEX is outside this wiki's primary scope but listed for completeness — many FLL alumni progress to VEX IQ or V5.

### VEXcode IQ

- **URL:** https://www.vexrobotics.com/iq/products/coding
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, teachers, families
- **Level:** beginner, intermediate
- **Tags:** vex, iq, official, programming, block-based, python
- **Use when:** Your team is in VEX IQ (the elementary/middle-school competition level).

Official VEX programming environment for VEX IQ. Block-based and Python. Browser-based or desktop.

### VEXcode V5

- **URL:** https://www.vexrobotics.com/v5/products/coding
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** teens, coaches
- **Level:** intermediate, advanced
- **Tags:** vex, v5, vrc, official, programming, block-based, python, cpp
- **Use when:** Your team is in VEX V5 (the high-school VRC level).

Official VEX programming environment for VEX V5. Block-based, Python, and C++. Required for VRC competition.

### PROS

- **URL:** https://pros.cs.purdue.edu
- **Authority:** independent
- **Audience:** teens, coaches
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** vex, v5, cpp, alternative, free, open-source
- **Use when:** A V5 team wants real C++ with full control over scheduling and a proper toolchain — Purdue's open-source alternative to VEXcode V5.

Open-source C++ framework for VEX V5 from Purdue. Used by many top-tier VRC teams. Steeper learning curve than VEXcode V5; in exchange, a more powerful toolchain and debugger.

## Design tools

### OnShape (CAD)

- **URL:** https://www.onshape.com/en/education
- **Authority:** independent
- **Audience:** teens, coaches
- **Level:** intermediate, advanced
- **Tags:** cad, design, free-for-education
- **Use when:** A team wants to CAD attachments or document robot designs digitally. Free for students and educators with appropriate affiliation.

Browser-based parametric CAD. Free education tier. Common in robotics for designing attachments before building. FLL teams typically don't need CAD — pencil sketches usually suffice — but for older teams iterating on 3D-printed parts or VEX teams designing metal subassemblies, it is the go-to.

## Notebooks, presentations, and team workflow

These aren't FLL/VEX-specific tools so we don't list them as entries, but they appear in nearly every team's workflow:

- **Engineering notebook**: paper, Google Docs, or Notion all work fine — pick one and start it the week of kickoff.
- **Innovation Project presentations**: Google Slides, Canva, or Keynote.
- **Code versioning** (advanced teams): GitHub or any git host.
- **Match timer**: any phone timer; or a dedicated FLL/VEX coach timer app from the relevant app store.
