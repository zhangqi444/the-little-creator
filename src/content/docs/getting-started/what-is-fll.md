FIRST Lego League is a global robotics program for kids aged **9–16** that combines robot building, programming, and a real-world research project — all in one season.

## The Three Parts of FLL

Every FLL season has three components judged at competitions:

**1. Robot Game**
Teams design, build, and program a LEGO Mindstorms or SPIKE Prime robot to complete missions on a themed game board. Missions are worth different points, and strategy matters as much as engineering.

**2. Innovation Project**
Teams identify a real-world problem related to the season's theme, research it, design a solution, and present it to judges. This is where kids learn to think like scientists and engineers — not just builders.

**3. Core Values**
FLL emphasizes teamwork, discovery, inclusion, and gracious professionalism. Judges evaluate how your team works together, not just what you build.

## The Season Cycle

- **August** — New season theme and challenge revealed
- **Aug–Nov** — Build season: design, program, iterate
- **Nov–Dec** — Qualifying tournaments
- **Jan–Apr** — Regional and world championship (for qualifying teams)

## What You Need to Get Started

- A team of 2–10 kids
- A LEGO SPIKE Prime or Mindstorms set (official FLL kit recommended)
- A coach (usually a parent — no engineering degree required!)
- Registration through [FIRST](https://www.firstlegoleague.org)

## Programming in FLL

FLL robots are programmed using **LEGO SPIKE App** (block-based) or **Python** via the SPIKE Prime hub. Most beginners start with blocks and graduate to Python within a season or two.
