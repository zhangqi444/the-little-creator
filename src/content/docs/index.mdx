## Six programs, one community

<CardGrid stagger>
  <Card title="FIRST LEGO League (FLL)" icon="rocket">
    Three divisions for ages 4–16: Discover, Explore, and Challenge. SPIKE Prime robots, Innovation Project, Core Values — and the most newcomer-friendly entry point into competitive robotics.
    [What is FLL? →](/getting-started/what-is-fll/)
  </Card>
  <Card title="FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC)" icon="puzzle">
    Grades 7–12. REV Robotics hardware, FTC SDK (Java/Kotlin), alliance play, and hands-on engineering portfolio judging. The natural next step from FLL Challenge.
    [What is FTC? →](/getting-started/what-is-ftc/)
  </Card>
  <Card title="FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC)" icon="star">
    Grades 9–12. The flagship FIRST program — full-scale robots, 25+ student teams, WPILib (Java/C++), and a game field that changes every season.
    [What is FRC? →](/getting-started/what-is-frc/)
  </Card>
  <Card title="VEX IQ" icon="heart">
    Ages 8–14. Plastic snap-together kit, VEXcode IQ Python, collaborative match format. Great for the middle-school bridge between FLL and VRC.
    [What is VEX IQ? →](/getting-started/what-is-vex-iq/)
  </Card>
  <Card title="VEX V5 / VRC" icon="document">
    Grades 8–12. Metal construction, V5 Brain, PROS C++ or VEXcode, solo competitive matches. VRC is the VEX flagship for high school.
    [What is VEX V5? →](/getting-started/what-is-vex-v5/)
  </Card>
  <Card title="VEX U" icon="open-book">
    College. No size or weight limits, PROS C++, two robots per team — the most open-ended VEX format.
    [What is VEX U? →](/getting-started/what-is-vex-u/)
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## Our story

The Little Creator started as one family's journey into FIRST LEGO League. We quickly realized the hardest part isn't the robots — it's finding good information, supportive teammates, and a community that gets it. So we built one.

We've grown to cover all six major youth competitive robotics programs: FLL, FTC, FRC, VEX IQ, VEX V5, and VEX U. Whether you're a first-year FLL parent-coach, a teacher running a robotics club, a teen writing FTC Java, or a college team competing in VEX U — there's something here for you.
