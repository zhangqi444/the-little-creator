Single-day [workshop formats](/for-educators/workshop-formats/) give a taste. A multi-day camp gives students enough time to iterate — the heart of engineering. These outlines stack day-by-day so each session builds on the previous one, ending with a genuine demonstration the students have earned.

Use these for:

- **Summer robotics camps** (school-hosted, library, community centre, or independent)
- **Break-week intensives** (winter or spring break)
- **School STEM weeks** where one full week is dedicated to a program
- **Team onboarding** before a new season starts

---

## How to read these outlines

Each outline specifies:

- **Duration** — 3-day, 4-day, or 5-day track; each day is a 3–4 hour session unless noted
- **Age range and program** — target audience and which robotics program
- **Kit / hardware** — what must be in the room before Day 1
- **Win condition** — what every student should be able to show at the end of the final day
- **Daily structure** — morning/afternoon blocks or timed agenda

All outlines assume one lead facilitator per 12–16 students. Add a second facilitator for larger groups or programs with heavier hardware setup (FRC, VEX V5 full robot).

Adapt freely — these are starting points, not scripts. If a group moves faster, extend the open build time. If Day 2 hardware trouble eats an hour, shift the Day 3 agenda.

## FLL — 3-Day SPIKE Prime Challenge Camp

**Duration:** 3 days x 3 hours | **Ages:** 9–14 | **Group size:** up to 18 (pairs/trios) | **Energy:** medium-high

**Kit required:**

- One SPIKE Prime starter set per pair or trio
- Laptops with SPIKE App 3.x installed and Bluetooth pairing verified
- A printed or projected FLL mission mat (any recent season) or a simple tape-marked table course
- Optional: FLL season game card or printed mission list

**Win condition:** Each team drives a programmed robot through at least two missions (or equivalent timed tasks) on a shared mat and can explain one design decision they iterated on.

### Day 1 — Hardware and First Drive (3 hours)

| Time | Block | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | **Welcome** | Introduce the camp format, the day's goal, and team pairings. 10 min. |
| 0:10 | **Hardware tour** | Identify SPIKE Prime hub, ports, motors, colour/distance/force sensors. Each pair turns on their hub and confirms connection. |
| 0:30 | **Build drive base** | Follow reference instructions. Facilitator circulates; let pairs work through misaligned axles before offering help. |
| 1:00 | **First program** | SPIKE App: new project, Blocks, Drive Forward 20 cm, Run. Every pair gets the robot moving before the break. |
| 1:20 | **Break** | 10 min. |
| 1:30 | **Turning and control** | Add a Turn block. Mini-challenge: navigate a simple L-shaped tape course. |
| 2:00 | **Intro to missions** | Show the mat (or tape course). Explain missions as scored tasks. Teams pick one mission to attempt tomorrow. |
| 2:30 | **Reflect and notebook intro** | What did you build? What did not work? Why? Each team writes 3 sentences in a notebook. Habit starts on Day 1. |
| 2:50 | **Wrap-up** | Leave robots in a named tray. Brief preview of Day 2 goal. |

### Day 2 — Sensors and Mission Attempts (3 hours)

| Time | Block | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | **Warm-up** | 5-minute recap: what was hard yesterday? Teams share one fix they want to try. |
| 0:05 | **Sensor programming** | Colour sensor: stop on line. Distance sensor: stop before wall. Each pair programs both. |
| 0:45 | **Mission attempt 1** | Teams run their chosen mission. Facilitator scores attempts on a whiteboard. |
| 1:10 | **Break** | 10 min. |
| 1:20 | **Iteration loop** | What broke? Adjust the program or the attachment. Three more attempts. Facilitator asks "why did you change that?" rather than "try this." |
| 2:00 | **Second mission** | Teams pick a second mission. First attempt. |
| 2:30 | **Notebook update** | Update design notes: what changed since yesterday and why. |
| 2:45 | **Reflect** | Whole group: what is harder, building or programming? Why? |
| 2:50 | **Wrap-up** | Preview Day 3 demo format. |

### Day 3 — Polish, Strategy, and Showcase (3 hours)

| Time | Block | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | **Free practice** | 45 min unstructured mission time. Teams decide their own priorities. |
| 0:45 | **Strategy session** | Which two missions are you running in the demo? In what order? Why? Teams plan their run sequence on paper. |
| 1:00 | **Final prep** | Last programming changes and attachment checks. No new missions. |
| 1:20 | **Break** | 10 min. |
| 1:30 | **Showcase runs** | Each team completes a timed 2.5-minute run. Facilitator or peers score. |
| 2:00 | **Team debrief** | What would you change with one more day? What are you most proud of? |
| 2:20 | **Awards or certificates** | Optional: "Best Score," "Most Improved," "Best Strategy Explanation" — keeps it celebratory, not competitive. |
| 2:40 | **Close** | Resources for next steps: joining a team, starting a team, the FLL season calendar. |

**Facilitator notes:**

- Pre-build one complete drive base as a reference model. Do not use it to demo — use it only when a pair is completely stuck and needs a physical reference.
- The notebook habit (Day 1 close, Day 2 close) is the highest-ROI five minutes of the camp. Do not skip it, even when time is tight.
- For ages 9–10 in the same group as 13–14: pair younger with older, not younger with younger. Mixed-age pairs outperform same-age pairs in almost every observable measure.

## FLL — 5-Day Full Challenge Camp

**Duration:** 5 days x 3.5 hours | **Ages:** 10–16 | **Group size:** up to 20 (teams of 2–3) | **Energy:** medium-high

**Kit required:**

- One SPIKE Prime set and laptop per pair
- Full FLL mat or equivalent table course
- Poster paper and markers for Innovation Project day

**Win condition:** Each team completes a scored robot run (3+ missions) and delivers a 3-minute Innovation Project pitch.

### Day-by-day overview

| Day | Theme | Key output |
|-----|-------|------------|
| Day 1 | Hardware and first drive | Robot driving, notebook started |
| Day 2 | Sensors and first mission | Two missions attempted |
| Day 3 | Innovation Project | 3-min pitch delivered |
| Day 4 | Strategy and iteration | Mission sequence planned and practiced |
| Day 5 | Showcase | Scored robot run and pitch replay |

**Day 3 — Innovation Project (3.5 hours):**

| Time | Block | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | **Intro** | What is the FLL Innovation Project? Show a 2-min example video. |
| 0:15 | **Problem brainstorm** | Each team picks a real-world problem from a list (or their own). Sticky-note ideas on poster paper. |
| 0:45 | **Solution design** | Sketch a proposed solution. What does it do? Who does it help? What would you build or change? |
| 1:15 | **Expert brainstorm** | If you could interview one expert, who? What would you ask? |
| 1:30 | **Break** | 10 min. |
| 1:40 | **Pitch prep** | Structure: problem, solution, expert, next step. 3 minutes total. |
| 2:10 | **Pitch rehearsal** | Teams deliver to another team. Feedback: one thing they explained clearly, one question you still have. |
| 2:30 | **Revise** | 20 min to update poster/notes based on feedback. |
| 2:50 | **Debrief** | How is designing a solution different from programming a robot? |
| 3:20 | **Close** | Preview Day 4 robot strategy session. |

Days 1, 2, 4, and 5 follow the same structure as the 3-Day camp, with extra time for iteration.

## FTC — 4-Day Rookie Build Camp

**Duration:** 4 days x 4 hours | **Ages:** 12–17 | **Group size:** up to 16 (teams of 4) | **Energy:** medium-high

**Kit required:**

- One REV Starter Kit or goBILDA drivetrain kit per team
- Control Hub and Driver Hub per team, charged
- Laptops with Android Studio or OnBot Java installed and USB drivers confirmed
- FTC season game field tile (or tape-marked floor space, approximately 12 ft x 12 ft)

**Win condition:** Each team drives a TeleOp-controlled mecanum chassis around a field perimeter and runs a 5-second encoder-based Autonomous program.

### Day-by-day overview

| Day | Theme | Key output |
|-----|-------|------------|
| Day 1 | Hardware and drive base build | Mecanum drive base assembled and wired |
| Day 2 | TeleOp programming and first drive | TeleOp working; robot drives under controller |
| Day 3 | Autonomous and Engineering Portfolio | Encoder auto runs; Portfolio Day 1 started |
| Day 4 | Polish and showcase | Timed field run and Portfolio intro presented |

### Day 1 — Drive Base Build (4 hours)

| Time | Block | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | **Intro** | FTC program overview, season format, scoring example. 15 min. |
| 0:15 | **Hardware inventory** | Identify all kit parts: chassis rails, mecanum wheels, REV motors, Control Hub, wiring. |
| 0:45 | **Build session** | Teams build mecanum drive base from kit instructions. Facilitator prioritises: axle alignment, then wheel orientation (correct roller angle matters for mecanum). |
| 2:00 | **Wiring** | Motor wiring to Control Hub ports. Facilitator demonstrates before teams wire independently. |
| 2:45 | **Power-on** | Connect Driver Hub, pair controllers, verify Control Hub LEDs. |
| 3:00 | **Break and trouble ticket** | Teams write one unresolved mechanical or electrical issue on the whiteboard. |
| 3:10 | **Resolve and close** | Facilitator addresses top issues. Preview: tomorrow we write the first program. |
| 3:45 | **Notebook** | Hardware setup notes: what is wired to what port, what the team had to fix. |

### Day 2 — TeleOp Programming and First Drive (4 hours)

| Time | Block | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | **SDK orientation** | Android Studio (or OnBot Java): project structure, OpMode, @TeleOp annotation, gamepad. 20 min. |
| 0:20 | **Mecanum drive code** | Provide a starter TeleOp skeleton; teams fill in the motor power calculations (field-centric or robot-centric; choose one per team). |
| 1:00 | **First deploy and drive** | Flash and drive. Facilitator focuses on "does it strafe?" as the mecanum success test. |
| 1:30 | **Debug loop** | Motor reversal, power scaling, controller deadband. Each fix logged in notebook. |
| 2:00 | **Break** | 10 min. |
| 2:10 | **Driver practice** | 30 min structured driving: figure-eight course, parking zone, simulated pick-up zone. |
| 2:40 | **Programming refinement** | Add telemetry. Show Driver Hub telemetry view. |
| 3:10 | **Team reflection** | What would you change on the drive base tomorrow? |
| 3:30 | **Notebook** | TeleOp code notes and driver observations. |

### Day 3 — Autonomous and Engineering Portfolio (4 hours)

| Time | Block | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | **Autonomous concepts** | OpMode, @Autonomous annotation, encoder ticks per inch. 20 min. |
| 0:20 | **Encoder auto code** | Write a 5-second autonomous: drive forward 24 in., turn 90 degrees, drive forward 12 in. |
| 1:00 | **First auto run** | Teams run on field. Measure landing position vs. intended. |
| 1:20 | **Calibration loop** | Tune ticks-per-inch, turn correction. Goal: land within 6 inches. |
| 2:00 | **Break** | 10 min. |
| 2:10 | **Engineering Portfolio intro** | What is the FTC Engineering Portfolio? Show a sample 2-page spread. What does good look like? |
| 2:30 | **Portfolio Day 1** | Teams document today's autonomous design: what they tried, what they measured, what they changed. Paper or Google Slides. |
| 3:00 | **Peer review** | Swap portfolios between teams. One thing explained clearly, one question. |
| 3:30 | **Notebook** | Autonomous tuning results and portfolio notes. |

### Day 4 — Polish and Showcase (4 hours)

| Time | Block | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | **Free build and drive** | 1 hour unstructured. Teams prioritise their own improvements. |
| 1:00 | **Showcase prep** | Plan the 5-minute demo sequence: autonomous run, then TeleOp perimeter run, then 2-min Portfolio walk-through. |
| 1:30 | **Break** | 10 min. |
| 1:40 | **Showcase runs** | Each team completes their planned demo in front of the group. Peers ask one question per team. |
| 2:30 | **Group debrief** | What is the difference between a robot that works in practice and a robot that works at a tournament? |
| 3:00 | **Next steps** | Joining an FTC team, season registration, Game Manual 0 resource, FTC Discord. |
| 3:30 | **Close and certificates** | |

## FRC — 5-Day Kickoff Simulation Camp

**Duration:** 5 days x 4 hours | **Ages:** 14–18 | **Group size:** up to 20 (two teams of 10) | **Energy:** medium-high

**Kit required:**

- Two WPILib practice drive bases (or AndyMark AM14U4 kits) with roboRIO, PDH, wired motors
- Driver Stations with FRC Driver Station software installed and radio programmed
- Laptops with WPILib and VS Code installed; verify Gradle builds offline before camp
- Floor space for a simplified FRC-scale field element (or at minimum a 10 x 10 ft open practice area)

**Win condition:** Each team deploys a Command-based TeleOp and a 5-second encoder Autonomous, conducts a pit-style interview on their design decisions, and delivers a 90-second Impact pitch.

### Day-by-day overview

| Day | Theme | Key output |
|-----|-------|------------|
| Day 1 | Hardware orientation and drive | Drive base understood; first TeleOp drive |
| Day 2 | Command-based framework | DriveSubsystem and JoystickDriveCommand working |
| Day 3 | Autonomous and Shuffleboard | Encoder auto runs; Shuffleboard telemetry displayed |
| Day 4 | Impact Award and strategy | 90-sec Impact pitch delivered; strategy session run |
| Day 5 | Scrimmage and showcase | Two-team scrimmage; pit interview and Impact delivery |

### Day 4 — Impact Award and Strategy (4 hours)

The FRC Impact Award is the most prestigious award and is submitted months before the event. Day 4 introduces the concept so teams begin thinking about it:

| Time | Block | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | **What is the Impact Award?** | 20 min: show examples of winning submissions; discuss what "measurable community impact" means vs. "we did community service." |
| 0:20 | **Team story brainstorm** | What has your team done for your school or community? List everything, including small things. |
| 0:50 | **Narrative structure** | Frame as: Why, What, How, Outcome. One story, not a list of events. |
| 1:20 | **90-second pitch draft** | Teams write and rehearse a 90-second verbal pitch. |
| 2:00 | **Break** | 10 min. |
| 2:10 | **Game strategy** | How do you build a strategy when you do not know the game yet? Prioritise: reliable Autonomous points, consistent TeleOp task, achievable endgame. |
| 2:40 | **Alliance strategy intro** | How FRC alliances form; role division between three robots; what good scouting finds. |
| 3:10 | **Impact pitch delivery** | Each team delivers their 90-second pitch to the other team. Peer feedback: one concrete outcome mentioned, one thing unclear. |
| 3:30 | **Revise and notebook** | Update pitch. Note what judges might probe. |

**Day 5 scrimmage format:**

- Two teams, simplified game rules (facilitator defines 2–3 scoring tasks the day before)
- Qualifying match (2 minutes): each team vs. the other, robot-centric
- Alliance match (2 minutes): teams ally; facilitator joins as third robot placeholder or runs 2v0 for pure strategy practice
- Pit interviews: two-minute stand-up at each robot, covering design decisions and one change made during the camp

## VEX IQ — 3-Day VIQRC Intro Camp

**Duration:** 3 days x 3 hours | **Ages:** 8–14 | **Group size:** up to 18 (pairs/trios) | **Energy:** medium-high

**Kit required:**

- One VEX IQ Super Kit per pair or trio
- Tablets or laptops with VEXcode IQ installed
- Tape-marked floor or table course simulating a simple VIQRC Teamwork Challenge field zone

**Win condition:** Each team drives a self-built robot through a Teamwork Challenge simulation (two teams cooperating on a shared field) and runs a 10-second Autonomous Coding Skills attempt.

### Day-by-day overview

| Day | Theme | Key output |
|-----|-------|------------|
| Day 1 | Build and first drive | Drive base built; controller drive confirmed |
| Day 2 | Sensors and Autonomous | Distance stop working; first ACS attempt |
| Day 3 | Teamwork Challenge simulation and showcase | Cooperative run completed; ACS score recorded |

### Day 2 — Sensors and Autonomous (3 hours)

| Time | Block | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | **Warm-up** | What did you fix overnight? Quick team share. |
| 0:10 | **Distance sensor** | Program: stop before wall. Each pair tests. |
| 0:40 | **Autonomous intro** | VEX IQ Autonomous Coding Skills — 60 seconds, robot only. Show a simple example. |
| 1:00 | **First ACS program** | Drive forward, turn 90 degrees, drive to object zone. Timed run and score recorded. |
| 1:30 | **Break** | 10 min. |
| 1:40 | **ACS iteration** | Two more attempts. Teams decide what to change; facilitator asks "why?" before approving any changes. |
| 2:10 | **Notebook** | Program notes and run results table (attempt, score, what changed). |
| 2:40 | **Preview** | Tomorrow we run the Teamwork Challenge with another team. Discuss cooperative vs. competitive mindset. |
| 2:50 | **Close** | |

### Day 3 — Teamwork Challenge and Showcase (3 hours)

Pair teams together. Each 2-team group cooperates on a shared field element (two robots, shared score). Emphasise GP: help the other team's robot, not just your own.

| Time | Block | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | **Practice with partner** | 45 min cooperative driving with paired team. |
| 0:45 | **Strategy session** | Which zones does each robot cover? How do you avoid bumping each other? |
| 1:00 | **Formal Teamwork run** | Timed 60-second run, scored by facilitator. |
| 1:20 | **ACS re-run** | Final ACS attempt for each team. Personal-best goal. |
| 1:40 | **Break** | 10 min. |
| 1:50 | **Showcase** | Teams present: robot, best ACS score, and one design decision they are proud of. |
| 2:20 | **GP debrief** | How did you help the other team? How is that different from what you would do in a real competition? |
| 2:40 | **Next steps** | RobotEvents season registration, finding a league. |
| 2:55 | **Close** | |

## VEX V5 — 4-Day VRC Strategy Camp

**Duration:** 4 days x 4 hours | **Ages:** 13–18 | **Group size:** up to 16 (teams of 4) | **Energy:** medium-high

**Kit required:**

- One VEX V5 Starter Kit (or equivalent 4-motor tank base) per team
- V5 Brain and controller per team, charged
- Laptops with VEXcode Pro V5 installed
- Tape-marked field area (at minimum half-field dimensions for strategy work)

**Win condition:** Each team drives a TeleOp tank drive, runs a 15-second encoder Autonomous, and presents a 3-minute strategy overview covering autonomous path, TeleOp zone assignment, and endgame plan.

### Day-by-day overview

| Day | Theme | Key output |
|-----|-------|------------|
| Day 1 | Build and first TeleOp drive | Tank drive assembled and driving |
| Day 2 | Autonomous and IMU | Encoder auto and gyro straight running |
| Day 3 | Game strategy and scouting | Written match strategy, 3-min strategy pitch |
| Day 4 | Scrimmage and showcase | Head-to-head match; strategy pitch delivered |

### Day 3 — Game Strategy and Scouting (4 hours)

| Time | Block | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | **VRC scoring overview** | Walk through a sample game (use a previous season or simplified fictional game): Autonomous Win Point, TeleOp scoring tasks, endgame. 20 min. |
| 0:20 | **Autonomous Win Point** | What is it worth? How reliable must your auto be to always claim it? Probability discussion: if it works 7/10 times, is that good enough? |
| 0:50 | **Zone assignment strategy** | Two robots, one field. Which zones does each robot own? Practice drawing zone maps on whiteboards. |
| 1:20 | **Endgame planning** | When do you start climbing/parking? How do you coordinate with partner? Role-play the last 30 seconds verbally. |
| 2:00 | **Break** | 10 min. |
| 2:10 | **Scouting intro** | What do you watch for in a match? Track: auto success rate, consistent task, endgame reliability. Use a simple paper scouting sheet. |
| 2:30 | **Scouting practice** | Show 2–3 recorded VRC match clips. Teams fill in scouting sheets. Debrief: what did you notice? |
| 3:00 | **Strategy pitch prep** | Each team writes their match strategy: autonomous path, TeleOp zone ownership, endgame trigger, communication cue for partner. |
| 3:30 | **Pitch delivery** | Each team delivers their 3-minute strategy to the other teams. Peers ask "what would you do if your auto fails?" |

## Adapting Across Programs

### Shared principles that work in every camp

**The win-condition rule:** Before each day, state the day's win condition out loud. If you cannot describe what every student should have done (not just learned) by the end of the day, restructure the session. Vague objectives produce vague outcomes.

**Iteration over completion:** The most valuable learning happens during the third attempt at something, not the first. Schedule free-iteration blocks generously. Cut extra content before cutting iteration time.

**Notebook as daily habit:** Every camp should end each day with 5–10 minutes of notebook writing. This is non-negotiable in FLL (Core Values/process evidence) and FTC/FRC (Engineering Portfolio), but it benefits every program as a reflection and knowledge-transfer tool. Start it on Day 1 so it normalises before the end of camp.

**Mixed-age pairing:** In camps covering an age range wider than 3–4 years, pair mixed-age wherever possible. Older students consolidate knowledge by explaining; younger students model effort from peers who are not that much older.

**The 70% rule for facilitators:** If a team is 70% of the way to fixing a problem themselves, do not intervene. Let them finish. Intervene when they are genuinely stuck (more than 5 min of no progress) or about to damage hardware.

### Scaling a camp up or down

| If you have | Adjust like this |
|---|---|
| 1 extra day | Add a second iteration day between the mid-camp and showcase days |
| 1 fewer day | Cut the strategy/award session and move the showcase into the final 45 min of the last session |
| Half the hardware | Run two rotations: half the group builds while the other half does planning/notebook work; swap at midday |
| Ages 8–10 only | Use the FLL 3-Day or VEX IQ 3-Day outlines; reduce programming complexity; keep motor count to 2 in the first session |
| Ages 16–18 only | Move faster; skip the "why" explanations for basic programming constructs; spend extra time on strategy and engineering documentation |
