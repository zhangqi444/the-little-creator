## REC Foundation & competition infrastructure

VEX hardware comes from VEX Robotics; VEX *competitions* are run by the Robotics Education & Competition Foundation (RECF), a separate non-profit. Many families don't realise the split — and miss out on RECF resources for grants, scholarships, mentor training, and rules clarifications. The entries below cover the RECF side.

### REC Foundation — program operator

- **URL:** https://recf.org
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** families, coaches, teachers, event partners
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** recf, vex-iq, vex-v5, vex-u, governance, official
- **Use when:** You need to understand who actually runs VEX competitions (vs. who makes the hardware), find program-wide policies, locate event partners, or access cross-program resources like grants and scholarships.

The Robotics Education & Competition Foundation runs all VEX competition programs — VEX IQ (VIQRC), V5 (V5RC), VEX U (VURC), VEX AI (VAIRC) — plus the Aerial Drone Competition (ADC) and the JROTC robotics program. RECF handles team registration policies, event partner accreditation, rules processes, mentor training, and the annual VEX Robotics World Championship. If you're an experienced FIRST family: think of RECF as VEX's counterpart to FIRST HQ.

### REC Library — per-program knowledge bases (pattern)

- **URL:** https://kb.roboticseducation.org
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, teams, event partners, parents
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** recf, kb, viqrc, v5rc, vurc, vairc, adc, rules, support
- **Use when:** You need RECF's official, searchable knowledge base for a specific program — registration steps, rules clarifications, getting help, event partner procedures.

RECF runs separate Zendesk-style knowledge bases for each program, on a templated subdomain pattern: `https://<program>-kb.recf.org/hc/en-us`. Verified subdomains:
`vgoc-kb` (VEX GO), `viqrc-kb` (VEX IQ), `v5rc-kb` (V5), `vurc-kb` (VEX U), `vairc-kb` (VEX AI), `adc-kb` (Aerial Drone), `grants-kb` (Grants). The general students/parents/volunteers KB lives at `kb.roboticseducation.org`. Each KB is the canonical source for that program's getting-started articles and policy questions — preferred over forum threads for "is this allowed" questions.

### New to Robotics — RECF landing page

- **URL:** https://recf.org/new-to-robotics/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** families, parents, new teachers
- **Level:** beginner
- **Tags:** recf, getting-started, new-families
- **Use when:** A family is brand-new to VEX (or to competitive robotics in general) and needs a one-page orientation from the program operator.

RECF's curated entry point for families with no prior robotics exposure. Walks through what the different VEX programs are, how a season works, what age groups they serve, and how to find a team or start one. Pairs well with our own [What is VEX?](/getting-started/what-is-vex/) overview.

### RECF Trainings — coach and event partner courses

- **URL:** https://recf.org/recf-trainings/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, teachers, event partners, volunteers
- **Level:** beginner, intermediate
- **Tags:** recf, coach-training, professional-development, volunteers
- **Use when:** You're a new coach, parent volunteer, or event partner who wants structured RECF-run training on running a team or running an event.

Free RECF-delivered training tracks for adults supporting VEX teams — covers role-by-role onboarding (new coach, event partner, head referee, judge advisor, scorekeeper) and seasonal updates each game year. The "new coach" and "judge advisor" tracks are useful even if you'll never serve as an event partner — they teach how the season actually works behind the scenes.

## VEX U & advanced programs

### VEX U Robotics Competition (VURC)

- **URL:** https://recf.org/teams/competition/vex-u-robotics-competition/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** university students, advanced teens (dual-enrollment), college coaches
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** vex-u, vurc, university, college, advanced
- **Use when:** You're a college or university student, a high schooler curious about post–high school robotics, or a parent of a competitive student looking at the long-term pathway.

VEX U is the university-level VEX competition — over 200 teams worldwide. It uses the same V5 hardware platform as V5RC but lifts many of the build restrictions (machined parts, custom electronics in some categories), giving college teams room for ambitious mechanical and software designs. Plays the same seasonal game as V5RC (currently *Push Back* for 2025-26, transitioning to *Override* for 2026-27), with VEX U-specific rule modifications. Teams compete year-round at regional and national events and qualify for the VEX Robotics World Championship.

### VEX AI Robotics Competition (VAIRC)

- **URL:** https://recf.org/teams/competition/vex-ai-robotics-competition/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** advanced teens, university students
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** vex-ai, vairc, machine-vision, autonomous, advanced
- **Use when:** A team wants a fully-autonomous competition (no driver) using computer vision and machine learning — separate event from VRC/VURC.

VAIRC is RECF's autonomous-only competition: robots use cameras and onboard inference to navigate, identify game elements, and score without a human driver. Open to high school and university teams. Significantly more software-heavy than V5RC. Not the right starting point for a new team — recommended only after a season of V5 or VEX U experience.

### Aerial Drone Competition (ADC) — RECF-adjacent

- **URL:** https://recf.org/teams/competition/aerial-drone-competition/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** middle school + high school teams, coaches
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Tags:** adc, drone, recf, aerial, alternative-program
- **Use when:** A team is interested in flight rather than ground robots, or you want a sibling program to VEX with shared RECF infrastructure.

RECF runs the Aerial Drone Competition (ADC) as a parallel program using CoDrone EDU drones. Not VEX hardware, but shares the same season structure, event-partner network, scoring conventions, and World Championship venue as VEX programs. Worth knowing about — some families end up with one kid in VEX and another in ADC at the same tournament weekend.

## Funding & student opportunities

### Team Grants — RECF

- **URL:** https://recf.org/teams/for-participants/grants/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** new coaches, teachers, parents starting a team
- **Level:** beginner
- **Tags:** recf, grants, funding, equipment, startup-costs
- **Use when:** You're starting a new team or under-resourced school program and need help affording the initial hardware investment.

RECF runs a Team Grant Program that matches schools and community organisations with sponsor-funded resources — typically a free or discounted starter kit. Eligibility is sponsor-dependent (some grants are region-specific, some target Title I schools, some target underserved students). Apply early in the season planning cycle; awards are not guaranteed. The full grant catalogue lives at the [Grants knowledge base](https://grants-kb.recf.org/hc/en-us). See also our [Team Funding guide](/guides/team-funding/) for non-RECF funding ideas.

### Scholarships — RECF partner universities

- **URL:** https://recf.org/teams/for-participants/scholarships/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** competitive teens, parents of teens, coaches
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Tags:** recf, scholarships, college, university
- **Use when:** A high school student is approaching college applications and has VEX (or other RECF program) participation on their record.

RECF maintains a directory of more than 100 universities and colleges that offer scholarships specifically for students with RECF competition history — collectively, the foundation cites over $110M available. Awards vary by school (some are RECF-specific, some are general STEM with VEX as a qualifier). Worth reviewing two years before college applications so the student can align activities (Excellence Award, leadership roles) with what specific schools weight.

### Girl Powered — RECF initiative

- **URL:** https://recf.org/teams/for-participants/girl-powered/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, parents of girls, all-girls teams
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** recf, girl-powered, diversity, gender, workshops
- **Use when:** You coach an all-girls team, a co-ed team trying to recruit and retain girls, or want to host an outreach workshop introducing robotics to girls who haven't tried it yet.

RECF's Girl Powered initiative (presented by Google) funds workshops, awards, and structural support for getting and keeping more girls in robotics. Includes a workshop hosting kit, a separate Girl Powered Award at most tournaments, a scholarship track, and downloadable outreach materials. Useful framework even for teams that aren't all-girls — the inclusion principles transfer.

## Major events

### VEX Robotics World Championship

- **URL:** https://recf.org/vex-robotics-world-championship/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** qualifying teams, coaches, families considering the pathway
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** vex-worlds, championship, recf, qualification
- **Use when:** Your team is approaching qualification, you're planning a multi-year season strategy, or you're a new family curious what "the top" of VEX looks like.

The annual culmination of every VEX competition program (VIQRC, V5RC, VURC, VAIRC) plus the ADC — held over roughly 10 days in late April. Recent edition: 2026 in St. Louis, April 21–30. Qualification is regional and program-specific; check the [VURC](https://vurc-kb.recf.org/hc/en-us), [V5RC](https://v5rc-kb.recf.org/hc/en-us), or [VIQRC](https://viqrc-kb.recf.org/hc/en-us) knowledge bases for your program's path. Even if your team isn't competing, the [VEX TV](https://www.vexworlds.tv) live stream is free and worth showing kids — it makes the long-term pathway concrete.

### RECF Signature Events

- **URL:** https://recf.org/teams/competition/recf-signature-events/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** competitive teams, coaches
- **Level:** intermediate, advanced
- **Tags:** recf, signature-events, qualifier, regional
- **Use when:** A team wants exposure beyond local tournaments — Signature Events draw teams from multiple regions and use official branded fields.

RECF designates a small number of regional tournaments per season as "Signature Events" — larger, multi-region competitions hosted by experienced event partners. They use officially branded fields and award banners, often draw 60–80+ teams, and are a step up in scale from a typical local tournament. Useful intermediate goal between local and World Championship.

Community-maintained C++ library for PROS providing Odometry, PID, and Pure Pursuit for VEX V5 autonomous. Not relevant for VEX IQ or beginning teams.

## Progression pathway

VEX has a clear age-based progression:

| Program | Ages / Grades | Hardware | Next step |
|---|---|---|---|
| VEX GO | K–5 | Snap plastic, simple motors | VEX IQ |
| VEX IQ (VIQC) | 8–14 / grades 3–8 | Snap plastic, smart motors | VEX V5 |
| VEX V5 (VRC) | 11–18 / grades 7–12 | Metal + pneumatics | VEX U / FRC |
| VEX U | University | Full custom | — |

Most families coming from FLL move to **VEX IQ** first (ages 8–14), then optionally step to **VEX V5** in middle or high school. See [What is VEX?](/getting-started/what-is-vex/) for a fuller overview.
