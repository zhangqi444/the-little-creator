FLL registration has two layers that both need to happen: **national** (through FIRST) and **regional** (through your state or country's partner). They're separate systems and you typically need both.

:::tip[Which season is open?]
Registration for a new season usually opens in **spring or summer** before the August kickoff. If you're reading this in May–July, new-season slots may just be opening. The FIRST Dashboard is your live source of truth — if the current season isn't listed yet, check back in a week or two.
:::

## The registration hierarchy

Understanding who runs what saves a lot of confusion:

| Layer | Who | What they control |
|---|---|---|
| **Global** | FIRST HQ (firstlegoleague.org / firstinspires.org) | Program rules, national registration, season materials |
| **Regional / state** | Program Delivery Organization (PDO) | Local events, qualifying tournaments, state-specific fees, advancement |
| **Local** | Program Delivery Partner or club | Team support, scrimmages, local mentors |

**The key rule:** season dates, registration deadlines, and fees that apply to *your* region come from *your* regional partner — not from a different country's partner site. A date on a Germany/Austria/Switzerland (DACH) partner site, a UK partner site, or a Texas partner site is specific to *that* region.

## Step 1 — National registration (FIRST Dashboard)

Create or log into your account at [my.firstinspires.org](https://my.firstinspires.org).

1. **Create a FIRST account** if you don't have one (free).
2. Go to **My Teams → Register a new team**.
3. Choose **FLL Challenge** as the program and the upcoming season year.
4. Pay the national registration fee (check [firstinspires.org/programs/cost-and-registration](https://www.firstinspires.org/programs/cost-and-registration) for current pricing — it varies by program and region).
5. Add at least **two coaches** (FLL requires two screened adults per team).
6. Complete the **Youth Protection Program (YPP)** screening for all adult team members — this is mandatory and takes a few days.

Your team gets a **FIRST team number** after this step. You will need it for regional registration.

:::note[Can't find the next season yet?]
If the upcoming season isn't listed in the dashboard, the national registration window hasn't opened yet. Check the [FIRST FLL season page](https://www.firstinspires.org/programs/fll/game-and-season) for the announced open date, or sign up for FIRST's email list to get notified.
:::

## Step 2 — Regional registration

After your national team number is assigned, register with your **regional Program Delivery Organization (PDO)**. This is a separate step from national registration — most regions have their own fee, event registration, and local requirements.

**How to find your PDO:**

1. Go to [firstinspires.org/find-local-support](https://www.firstinspires.org/find-local-support).
2. Enter your zip code or state.
3. Look for your state's listed **Program Delivery Organization** — this is who to contact.

Some regional partners also have their own team registration portal that links back to your FIRST Dashboard team number.

### If you are in Washington state

Your regional partner is **FIRST Washington** — [firstwa.org](https://firstwa.org).

- **FLL Challenge WA overview:** [firstwa.org/fll-challenge/](https://firstwa.org/fll-challenge/) — team requirements (ages 9–14, grades 4–8, 2–10 members, 2 coaches minimum) and program overview.
- **WA challenge registration:** [firstwa.org/challenge-registration/](https://firstwa.org/challenge-registration/) — the actual sign-up form and local fee.
- **WA events:** [firstwa.org/events/](https://firstwa.org/events/) — qualifying tournaments and championship dates once the season opens.

FIRST Washington manages WA-specific event scheduling, qualifying tournament slots, and any local financial assistance programs.

## Step 3 — Tournament registration

Qualifying tournament spots open separately, usually **August–October** after kickoff. In Washington, these are listed on the [FIRST Washington events calendar](https://firstwa.org/events/) and often require registering through [RobotEvents](https://www.robotevents.com).

Pick a tournament that works for your team's schedule. If your region offers multiple qualifiers, earlier isn't always better — you want time to be competition-ready.

## Can I register through a different state's PDO?

Generally, teams should register in the state where they are based. Cross-registration (for example, a Washington team competing at a Montana event) depends on both states' PDOs agreeing and on whether the team is eligible for awards and advancement or must compete as exhibition-only. **Ask both PDOs before assuming you can transfer** — eligibility for advancement is region-specific.

## Timing overview

| What | When (approximate) |
|---|---|
| Next season announced | January–February |
| FIRST Dashboard opens for new season | Spring (varies by year) |
| Regional registration opens | Spring–summer (check your PDO) |
| Season kickoff / challenge reveal | August |
| Build + practice season | August–November |
| Qualifying tournaments | November–December |
| Regional/state championships | January–February |

These are typical ranges — your PDO sets the binding dates for your region. Do not rely on another country's registration calendar for U.S. planning.

## Registration checklist

- [ ] FIRST account created at my.firstinspires.org
- [ ] FLL Challenge team registered for the upcoming season in the FIRST Dashboard
- [ ] National registration fee paid
- [ ] Two or more adult coaches added to the team
- [ ] YPP screening completed for all adult members
- [ ] Your state PDO identified via firstinspires.org/find-local-support
- [ ] Regional registration completed with your PDO
- [ ] Qualifying tournament slot registered (opens after August kickoff)
