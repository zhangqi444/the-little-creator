FIRST LEGO League has three divisions. The right one depends primarily on your child's **age at the start of the season** (typically August/September). Grade level is a secondary guide — use it to confirm, not to override age.

:::tip[Quick answer]
- Child is **4–5**: FLL Discover
- Child is **6–8**: FLL Explore
- Child is **9–10**: either Explore or Challenge — see the overlap section below
- Child is **11–14** (US/Canada) or **11–15** (elsewhere): FLL Challenge
:::

---

## The three divisions at a glance

| Division | Age range | Grade equivalent (US) | Format | Competition? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Discover** | 4–6 | PreK–1st grade | DUPLO-based STEM play, guided activities | No — showcases only |
| **Explore** | 6–10 | 1st–5th grade | LEGO build + simple motor/sensor model, season theme | No — exhibitions only |
| **Challenge** | 9–14 (US/Canada)<br/>9–15 (elsewhere) | 4th–8th grade (US/Canada) | Autonomous robot + Innovation Project + Core Values | Yes — qualifying tournaments, regionals, championship |

**Sources:** Wikipedia citing FIRST, cross-referenced with firstlegoleague.org ("ages 5 to 16, age ranges vary by region") and firstinspires.org (FIRST LEGO League listed as "Grades K–8, Ages 5–16").

## The overlap zone: ages 9–10

Kids aged 9 or 10 are eligible for **either** Explore or Challenge. Both are valid — it comes down to readiness, not rules.

**Choose Explore if your child:**
- Has never done FLL before
- Is on the younger end (just turned 9)
- Prefers low-pressure, exhibition-style events over head-to-head competition
- Wants to build and present without the intensity of autonomous programming

**Choose Challenge if your child:**
- Has done Explore before (or equivalent robotics experience)
- Is comfortable with basic programming concepts
- Wants competitive tournaments
- Has a group of 2–10 kids their age ready to form a team

There is no shame in starting a 9-year-old in Explore — it's excellent preparation for Challenge the following year. Many coaches recommend it for students with no prior FLL or coding experience.

## Age cutoff: when is age measured?

FIRST measures age eligibility as of a specific cutoff date each season — typically **the date of the team's first tournament** or **January 1 of the competition year**, but **this varies by region and PDO**.

What this means practically:
- A child who turns 9 in October and whose first tournament is November is likely eligible for Challenge that same year
- A child who turns 15 in December may age out of Challenge before a late-season championship

**Always confirm the exact cutoff date with your regional PDO.** Do not rely on a general age range when planning for a child near the boundary.

---

## Upper age limit for Challenge

- **United States and Canada:** up to age **14** (some PDOs allow age 15 in the season year if the student is still in qualifying grade)
- **Rest of world:** up to age **15**
- **Grade cap (US):** typically grade 8 or equivalent — high school students (grade 9+) generally do not qualify even if under age, but PDOs have discretion

If your child is 14–15 and approaching the boundary, check directly with your PDO. Some regions allow age 15 if the student hasn't yet entered high school.

---

## Regional variations

The age ranges above are FIRST's global guidelines. Individual PDOs may narrow them slightly or apply different cutoff dates. Common regional differences:

- Some PDOs require the child to be within the age range for the **entire season**, not just at tournament time
- Some international regions (particularly Europe) use school year cohorts instead of exact ages
- A few PDOs set a **minimum team age average** — confirm if registering a team with wide age spread

**When in doubt:** ask your PDO directly. Their registration page or contact email is the binding source for your region. Use [FIRST Find Local Support](https://www.firstinspires.org/find-local-support) to find your PDO.

## Progression path

Kids typically move through the divisions as they grow:

```
Discover (ages 4–6)
    ↓
Explore (ages 6–10)
    ↓
Challenge (ages 9–14/15)
    ↓
FIRST Tech Challenge / VEX (ages 12–18) ← once they age out of FLL
```

It's perfectly fine to skip Discover or Explore and start directly in Challenge if the child is age-eligible and ready. The curriculum builds on itself but doesn't require lower division completion.

## What to tell the GPT

If you're asking this assistant about which division your child should join, it helps to share:

1. **Child's current age** (not grade — age is what eligibility is based on)
2. **Country and state/region** (age cutoff rules vary by PDO)
3. **Prior experience** (any FLL, robotics, or programming background?)
4. **Preference:** competition vs. exhibition format?

With those four inputs, a recommendation becomes specific rather than generic.
