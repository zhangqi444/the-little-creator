VEX is a competitive robotics program for students from elementary school through university. Operated by the [Robotics Education & Competition Foundation (RECF)](https://www.recf.org/), it offers more engineering complexity than FLL and is a popular pathway toward high school robotics, engineering careers, and university competitions.

:::note[Current season]
The 2025-26 VEX season is **Push Back** for both VEX IQ (VIQRC) and VEX V5 (V5RC). The 2026-27 season, **Override**, was announced and transitions in summer 2026. Check the [VEX current game pages](https://www.vexrobotics.com/viqc-current-game) and the [VEX Resource Map](/resources/vex-resource-map/) for up-to-date season materials.
:::

## VEX Programs

| Program | Age | Grade | Hardware | Competition |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| VEX IQ | 8–14 | Elementary–Middle | Snap-together plastic (similar to LEGO) | VIQRC (VEX IQ Robotics Competition) |
| VEX V5 / VRC | 11–18 | Middle–High school | Metal components, V5 Brain | V5RC (VEX Robotics Competition) |
| VEX U | University | College | Full V5 platform + custom machined parts | VURC (VEX U Robotics Competition) |

For most families coming from FLL, **VEX IQ** is the natural next step. **VEX V5** (also called VRC — VEX Robotics Competition) is what most high school teams compete in. For more on the university-level program, see [What is VEX U?](/getting-started/what-is-vex-u/).

Not sure which level fits your student? See the [Progression Guide](/getting-started/progression-guide/) for a structured path from FLL through VEX IQ, V5, and beyond.

## How VEX Competitions Work

Each season introduces a new game played on a 12×12 ft field. Two alliances of two robots each compete head-to-head in qualification and elimination matches. Scoring depends on the game's objectives (stacking, scoring game objects, hanging, etc.).

Competitions also include a **Skills Challenge** where a single robot runs the field alone — first a Driver Skills run (operator-controlled), then a Programming Skills run (fully autonomous). Skills scores contribute to a separate global ranking and are a key metric at World Championship qualification.

Events are run by regional Event Partners and organized through [RobotEvents.com](https://www.robotevents.com) — the official platform for finding events and managing team registration. The season culminates at the **VEX Robotics World Championship**, typically held in late April.

For what to expect at your first competition day, see [Your First VEX Tournament — What to Expect](/guides/first-vex-tournament/).

## Awards

Beyond robot matches and skills scores, VEX tournaments award teams for excellence across multiple dimensions:

- **Excellence Award** — the headline award, spanning robot design, programming, skills, and team conduct
- **Design, Build, Think, Amaze, Innovate** — component awards recognizing specific strengths
- **Robot Skills Champion** — awarded for the highest combined skills score at an event

For the full breakdown of what each award measures and how to prepare, see [VEX Awards — What Each One Rewards](/guides/vex-awards/).

## Programming in VEX

### VEX IQ

VEX IQ uses the **V5 IQ Brain** (as of the second-generation hardware). Programming options include:

- **VEXcode IQ Blocks** — Drag-and-drop block programming, great for ages 8–12
- **VEXcode IQ Python** — Text-based Python, the natural progression from blocks
- **VEXcode IQ C++** — Available for advanced teams

Most IQ teams start with Blocks and move to Python as they gain confidence. See the [VEX IQ Programming Guide](/guides/vex-iq-programming/) for a practical intro.

### VEX V5

VEX V5 supports more advanced programming options suited to its older audience:

- **VEXcode Blocks** — Entry point, rarely used by competitive teams
- **VEXcode Pro V5 (C++)** — The standard for competitive V5 teams; motor control, sensor APIs, Competition template
- **PROS** — Open-source C++ framework used by top-tier teams; better tooling and community libraries

See the [VEX V5 Programming Basics guide](/guides/vex-v5-programming-basics/) for a complete intro to C++ and the Competition template.

## Key Differences from FLL

- **No research project.** VEX is purely robot competition — no Innovation Project or presentation judging (though the Excellence Award evaluates notebooks and team conduct).
- **More mechanical engineering.** Metal components, gears, pneumatics (in V5), and custom mounts vs. LEGO bricks.
- **Longer season, more events.** Multiple tournaments available regionally before state/national championship, unlike FLL's single local qualifier path.
- **Skills Challenge is unique.** FLL does not have an equivalent to the autonomous-only skills run. It's a parallel ranking system that travels across events.
- **Stronger pathway to high school and college robotics.** VEX IQ → VEX V5 → VEX U (and optionally FRC) is a well-worn path for students who want to continue through university.
