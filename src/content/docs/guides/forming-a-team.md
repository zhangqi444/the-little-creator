title: Forming a Team
description: How to find teammates, set expectations, and structure your robotics team for success.
---

One of the most common questions: *"How do we find teammates?"* Here's what actually works.

## Team Size

- **FLL**: 2–10 members (sweet spot is 4–6 for most families)
- **VEX IQ**: 2–3 students per robot is typical
- **VEX V5/VRC**: 4–8 students common at the high school level

Smaller teams mean every member carries more responsibility — which is great for learning, harder logistically. Larger teams need clearer roles.

## Where to Find Teammates

**Start with your immediate circle:**
- School classmates and friends who've expressed interest in coding or building
- Kids from science/math clubs at school
- Neighbors and neighborhood groups

**Go broader:**
- Post in local Facebook parent groups or Nextdoor
- Check if your school or library already has a robotics club
- Attend a local FLL/VEX scrimmage or tournament — other families there are your community

**Online:**
- GitHub Discussions right here — post in our [Find a Team](/community/find-a-team/) thread
- FIRST Community forums
- Reddit: r/FLL, r/VEX

## Setting Expectations Early

Before your first real practice, have a simple conversation with all families about:

- **Commitment level** — How many hours per week? How many weeks?
- **Meeting schedule** — When, where, how often?
- **Competition goal** — Are you competing to win, or competing to learn? (Both are valid, but misalignment causes conflict.)
- **Parent involvement** — Coaches coach, kids build. Define boundaries early.

## Roles to Assign

Even informal teams benefit from having loose roles:

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Driver(s) | Operates robot during match |
| Programmer | Writes and tests autonomous code |
| Builder | Mechanical design and construction |
| Researcher | FLL Innovation Project lead |
| Presenter | Speaks during judging sessions |

Kids can and should rotate roles across seasons to build well-rounded skills.
