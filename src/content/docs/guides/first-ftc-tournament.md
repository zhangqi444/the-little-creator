The emotional advice in that FLL guide transfers — first tournaments feel chaotic, telling the team that ahead of time helps — but the *structure* of an FTC tournament is meaningfully different from FLL, and your team will be better prepared if you know what's actually going to happen.

## How FTC tournaments are structured

A standard FTC tournament — typically called a **qualifier**, sometimes a **league meet** in league-format regions — runs as a single long day, usually 8am to 6pm. Teams play several **qualification matches**, scores aggregate into a ranking, the top-ranked teams form **alliances** for elimination matches, and elimination eventually produces winners. Awards are interleaved.

The high-level day looks like:

| Time (approx.) | What happens |
|---|---|
| 7:30–9:00 am | Check-in, pit setup, inspection |
| 9:00 am | Driver's meeting |
| 9:30 am | First judging interviews start |
| 10:00 am | Opening ceremony |
| 10:15 am – 1:30 pm | Qualification matches (round 1) + judging interviews continue |
| Lunch | Brief break (often 30 min); judging interviews may continue |
| 1:30 – 3:30 pm | Qualification matches (round 2), final judging |
| 3:30 pm | Alliance selection ceremony |
| 4:00 – 5:30 pm | Elimination matches (quarter / semi / finals) |
| 5:30 – 6:00 pm | Awards ceremony |

Times vary by region and number of teams. The schedule is the tournament's published source of truth — check it the night before for your specific match times and judging slot.

## The four things that will surprise a new FTC team

1. **The judging interview is a single 5–7 minute slot, and it's the only one you get.** Unlike FLL's three sessions (Innovation Project, Robot Design, Core Values), FTC has one combined interview where judges assess engineering process + outreach + team identity + Gracious Professionalism all in one window. Practice this hard.
2. **Alliance selection is dramatic.** After qualifying matches, the top 4 ranked teams become **alliance captains** and *pick* the other teams they want on their alliance — in order, across two rounds. Getting picked feels validating; not getting picked stings. Tell your team this is normal, not a referendum on whether they were "good."
3. **You'll play with strangers, not against them.** Each match is two alliances of two teams each. Your "ally" in match 3 might be your "opponent" in match 7. Adapt fast; coordinate quickly.
4. **Match strategy meetings happen in the pit between matches.** You'll have 10–15 minutes to walk over to your match partners, discuss who's doing what (autonomous routine, scoring focus, defense), and walk back. Have one person ready to be the alliance liaison.

## What "Inspection" actually checks

Robot inspection happens during the morning pit setup. An inspector goes through a checklist verifying your robot is *legal* to play:

- Size (fits in an 18"×18"×18" sizing cube when constrained)
- Mass (within the season's limit)
- Battery (charged, secured, on the right plug type)
- Wiring (powered through a properly-rated main switch, no exposed conductors)
- Markings (team number visible on both sides)
- Software (Driver Hub and Control Hub firmware versions, OpModes load without crashes)

Inspectors are *helpful*, not adversarial — their goal is to get you on the field. If you fail an item, they'll usually let you fix it and re-inspect. Bring electrical tape, a Phillips screwdriver, and your team number stickers to the inspection table.

## Awards: what gets handed out

FTC awards include the **Inspire Award** (the top award, recognising teams strong across engineering + outreach + Gracious Professionalism — qualifies to advance), the **Winning Alliance** (elimination-bracket winner), and several judged awards (Innovate, Connect, Think, Motivate, Control, Design, Compass, Promote) tied to specific aspects of team performance. See the [FTC Awards guide](/guides/ftc-awards/) for the full breakdown and what each one actually rewards.

## How to help your team navigate the day

**Before:**

- Walk through the schedule together the night before so everyone knows roughly where they should be when.
- Pack the night before, not the morning of.
- Charge two robot batteries, two Driver Hub units, and your laptop.

**During qualifying matches:**

- Rotate driver / coach / human-player roles if your roster allows, so multiple students get the field experience.
- Between matches, debrief in the pit — what worked, what didn't, what to change. Two minutes of structured reflection beats twenty minutes of stewing.
- Send one student to scout your upcoming opponents — what's their auto, what's their teleop pattern, what defensive style? This is fair game and expected.

**During judging:**

- Send your strongest two presenters, but bring at least three students so judges see team depth.
- Lead with your engineering process and *one* concrete example of an iteration cycle. Judges love specifics over generalities.
- Bring the Engineering Portfolio + notebook — see [FTC Engineering Portfolio](/guides/ftc-engineering-portfolio/).

**At alliance selection:**

- Before the ceremony, decide as a team: *if we get a pick, who's our first choice?* Have a ranked list.
- If you're not picked, hold your team's eyes — say something like "we're going to watch and learn" out loud. Mute the disappointment publicly; process it later, privately.

**After awards:**

- Whatever the outcome, do a 10-minute team debrief that night or the next day. What surprised us? What do we want to change before the next event? This is the highest-value habit a coach can install.

## Where this differs from FLL Challenge

| Aspect | FLL Challenge | FTC |
|---|---|---|
| Judging | Three separate sessions (Innovation Project, Robot Design, Core Values) | One combined 5–7 minute interview |
| Match format | One team per run | Two-team alliances |
| Strategy planning | Pre-set season-long | Per-match in the pit |
| Alliance selection | None | Yes, between qualifying and elimination |
| Robot size | 11"×11"×11" Mission Model area | 18"×18"×18" sizing cube |
| Tournament length | Often half-day | Full day |

## FTC-specific additions to the packing list

On top of the [general checklist](/guides/tournament-day-checklist/):

- **Two charged 12V batteries** (your match cadence is faster than FLL's; one battery isn't enough)
- **Spare Driver Hub** if you have one — a dead Driver Hub costs you a match
- **Engineering Portfolio printed** and in a binder (judges expect a physical copy)
- **Notebook** (separately from the Portfolio — the Portfolio summarises; the notebook supports)
- **Wire cutters, electrical tape, zip ties, M4 hex wrenches** — pit repairs are routine
- **A drive belt and a spare wheel** if your design depends on either
