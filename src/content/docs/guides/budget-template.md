Knowing what a season costs before it starts prevents the two most common team-management problems: running out of money in October, and parents being blindsided by expenses they didn't expect.

This guide gives you realistic cost ranges for all six programs, a line-by-line template you can copy into a spreadsheet, and notes on where teams consistently overspend or underspend.

For funding strategies (grants, sponsors, fundraising), see [Team Funding](/guides/team-funding/).

---

## Quick comparison: first-year cost ranges

| Program | Equipment (first year) | Registration | Travel (qualifier) | **Total first year** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **FLL Challenge** | $400–$600 | $275–$425 | $0–$500 | **$675–$1,525** |
| **FTC** | $1,200–$2,500 | $275–$350 | $0–$800 | **$1,475–$3,650** |
| **FRC** | $6,000–$12,000 | $5,000–$6,000 | $500–$3,000 | **$11,500–$21,000** |
| **VEX IQ** | $400–$700 | $100–$250 | $0–$400 | **$500–$1,350** |
| **VEX V5** | $1,500–$3,000 | $100–$300 | $0–$800 | **$1,600–$4,100** |
| **VEX U** | $3,000–$6,000 | $200–$500 | $200–$2,000 | **$3,400–$8,500** |

:::note
These are typical ranges for new teams in the United States. Costs vary by region, team size, whether you inherit equipment, and travel distance to events. Costs drop significantly in year two once equipment is owned. FRC costs in particular depend heavily on sponsorship — many FRC teams operate on $15,000–$50,000+ with sponsor support.
:::

## FLL Challenge budget template

Designed for a new team of 6–10 kids.

### One-time / first-year costs

| Line item | Typical cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| FIRST national registration | $225 | Confirm at [my.firstinspires.org](https://my.firstinspires.org); price may change each season |
| Regional/PDO registration | $50–$200 | Varies by state/region; some PDOs bundle fees |
| LEGO Education SPIKE Prime set | $350–$400 | Core kit; reused every season |

| Spare SPIKE Prime parts | $0–$100 | Useful but not required in year one |

### Recurring annual costs

| Line item | Typical cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|

| Regional/PDO registration | $50–$200 | Each season |
| FLL Challenge Set (new season) | $75–$125 | Replace each August |
| **Annual recurring total** | **$350–$550** | Equipment now amortised |

### Optional / variable costs

| Line item | Typical cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| T-shirts / team apparel | $100–$300 | Per team; common for tournament day |

| Extra batteries / charging dock | $50–$100 | Useful for long practice days |

## FTC budget template

Designed for a new team of 10–15 students. FTC costs vary widely — a resourceful team can start for under $1,500; a team buying everything new may spend $3,000+.

### One-time / first-year costs

| Line item | Typical cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| FIRST national registration | $275 | Per team |
| Regional registration | $0–$75 | Varies by region |
| Control system (REV Control Hub + Expansion Hub) | $400–$500 | Required; REV is most common |
| Drive motors (4–6 × REV HD Hex or goBILDA) | $200–$350 | REV HD Hex ~$40 each; goBILDA ~$50 |
| Drivetrain (chassis, wheels, hardware) | $200–$600 | REV Ion, goBILDA, or custom |
| Servo motors (2–6) | $100–$300 | REV Smart Servo ~$40 each |
| Sensors (distance, colour, IMU) | $50–$150 | Many sensors included with Control Hub |
| Battery + charger | $80–$150 | REV Slim Battery is standard |
| Android phone or REV Driver Hub | $50–$200 | REV Driver Hub (~$150) is now standard |
| Structural materials (REV extrusion, brackets) | $150–$400 | Season-dependent |
| **First-year total** | **$1,505–$2,725** | |

## FRC budget template

FRC is the highest-cost program. Most successful FRC teams rely on significant sponsorship. This template assumes a new team building its own fundraising base.

### One-time / first-year costs

| Line item | Typical cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Team registration (FIRST) | $5,000–$6,000 | Rookie registration currently $6,000 US; includes Kit of Parts |
| Kit of Parts (included with registration) | (included) | Provides drivetrain base, controllers, basic hardware |
| Additional drivetrain hardware | $300–$800 | West Coast Drive supplements, wheels, bearings |
| Motors beyond KOP (NEOs, Falcons, Krakens) | $300–$600 | ~$50–$100 each |
| Motor controllers (Spark MAX or Talon FX) | $200–$500 | ~$60–$90 each |
| Pneumatics (if used) | $200–$500 | Compressor, solenoids, cylinders |
| Structural materials (aluminium tubing, plates) | $300–$800 | Andymark, WCP, or local metal supplier |
| Electrical (PDP, breakers, wire, connectors) | $150–$300 | Most in KOP; supplements |
| Safety gear (glasses, gloves per member) | $50–$100 | |
| Tools (if team lacks a full shop) | $500–$2,000 | Drill press, saw, basic hand tools |
| **First-year total** | **$7,300–$11,600** | Excluding travel |

:::tip[FRC Kit of Parts]
The Kit of Parts (included in registration) significantly offsets equipment costs. New teams should carefully inventory the KOP before purchasing additional components.
:::

## Where teams consistently overspend

**Buying everything new when second-hand works.** Structural parts, wheels, and hardware can often be sourced from older kits or graduating teams. Check your program's community before buying new.

**Underestimating travel.** Hotels for a two-day FRC event or Worlds trip add up fast. Budget travel before the season, not after you've qualified.

**Apparel in year one.** Nice to have, but it's the first thing to cut when the budget is tight. Kids care more about competing than looking matching.

**Extra motors and servos without a specific design need.** Order what the robot design requires. Surplus parts are common at end-of-season donation tables.

**FRC teams not applying for the Rookie Kickstart grant.** FIRST offers grants specifically for new FRC teams. Apply before your first season — see [FIRST grants](https://www.firstinspires.org/community/grants).

---

## Where teams consistently underspend (and shouldn't)

**Spare batteries.** One battery per robot is always a risk at a tournament. Budget for two per robot.

**Safety gear.** Safety glasses for every team member who handles power tools or the robot during operation — non-negotiable for FTC, FRC, VEX V5, and VEX U.

**Basic communication tools.** A shared folder, messaging group, or notebook supplies are cheap and reduce season-long chaos. Budget $20–$50 even if you think you won't need it.

## Money-saving strategies

- **Inherit equipment** from a graduating team or disbanded program. Local Facebook groups, school robotics programs, and your PDO's community channels are good starting points.
- **Apply for grants before spending.** Many grants are awarded before equipment is purchased. Apply first, buy later. See [Team Funding](/guides/team-funding/) for known grant sources.
- **Split costs across families.** Some teams ask each family for a small flat contribution (e.g., $50) rather than chasing large sponsors — enough to cover consumables and apparel without a full fundraising campaign.
- **Use school resources.** Many schools cover registration fees if a robotics team is a school club. Printers, tools, and meeting space are often free for school-affiliated teams.
- **Borrow or rent field elements.** For FLL, some PDOs lend Challenge Sets to teams. Check before buying.
