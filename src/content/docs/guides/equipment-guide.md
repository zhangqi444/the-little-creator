One of the first questions new FLL families ask is: "Do we need to buy all this stuff?" The answer is nuanced — some things you must have, some you can share, and some you can borrow or get through your school or region. This guide breaks it down.

:::note[Prices and availability]
Hardware prices and availability change. The figures here are approximate as of 2025/26 — verify current pricing at [LEGO Education](https://education.lego.com) and [FIRST](https://www.firstlegoleague.org) before purchasing. Regional pricing varies.
:::

## What you actually need for FLL Challenge

### Required (no substitutes)

| Item | Approx. cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **LEGO Education SPIKE Prime Set** | ~$330–$380 USD | The official FLL robot kit. Includes the hub, motors, sensors, and LEGO elements. One per team — not per person. |
| **FLL Challenge Set** (seasonal) | ~$75–$100 USD | The game mat + mission models for the current season. Released each August. Must be ordered from FIRST or a regional partner. |
| **FIRST team registration** | ~$225 USD | Covers your team's entry into the official program, including access to the season materials and tournament eligibility. Fee varies by region. |

**Minimum total for a new team: roughly $600–$700 USD**, not including devices to run the programming software (laptop, tablet, or Chromebook).

### Strongly recommended

| Item | Approx. cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Extra SPIKE Prime rechargeable battery** | ~$30 USD | One battery lasts 2–3 hours. A spare means you never stop a practice session to charge. |
| **USB-A to USB-C cable** (or the SPIKE-specific cable) | ~$10 USD | More reliable than Bluetooth for uploading programs. Many teams have connection problems via Bluetooth only. |
| **Laptop or tablet** | Already own? | Any device that runs the free LEGO SPIKE App. Windows, macOS, iPadOS, Android, or Chromebook all work. |

### Optional but useful

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Extra LEGO pieces | SPIKE Prime comes with plenty for most builds, but some teams buy extra axles, gears, or specific elements for attachments. Source: LEGO Pick-a-Brick, BrickLink, or the LEGO Education shop. |
| Foam mat or table cover | Protects the game mat from scratches during practice. Not required, but extends mat life. |
| Team shirts | Not required — but kids love them and they help at tournaments. |
| Carrying case for the robot | A padded box or case reduces damage between practices and at tournaments. |

## Buy, borrow, or share?

### Borrow from your school or library

Many schools, libraries, and makerspaces already have SPIKE Prime sets. Before you buy:
- Ask your school's STEM coordinator or technology teacher
- Check with your local public library (many now loan SPIKE Prime kits)
- Contact your regional FLL affiliate — some loan or rent equipment to new teams

If you can borrow a set for a season before committing to buy, do it. It lets you confirm your team is committed before the investment.

### Share within a team

One SPIKE Prime set is enough for a team of up to 10 kids. You don't need one per person. Teams share the kit during build sessions — typically one sub-team works on the robot while others work on the Innovation Project.

If your team has more than ~6 kids and wants more hands-on robot time, a second set is useful but not required.

### Share between teams

If two families in your school or neighborhood are each starting a team, consider:
- Sharing one Challenge Set (game mat + missions) — teams don't need their own mat to practice, but it helps. Coordinate practice schedules.
- NOT sharing a SPIKE Prime set — each team needs their own robot to build and program independently.

### Buy used

SPIKE Prime sets can often be found used from teams that graduated members or disbanded. Sources:
- Local Facebook "Buy Nothing" or parent groups
- FLL community forums (search "used SPIKE Prime")
- eBay (verify it's the current SPIKE Prime, not the older EV3 or Mindstorms sets — they're not compatible with current FLL)

**Before buying used:** Verify all pieces are present, test that the hub charges and connects to the SPIKE App, and check that motors and sensors function.

## What NOT to buy (common mistakes)

### LEGO Mindstorms (any version)

LEGO discontinued Mindstorms. Previous FLL seasons used Mindstorms EV3, but current FLL Challenge requires **SPIKE Prime**. They are not compatible. Don't buy a used Mindstorms set expecting it to work for FLL Challenge.

### SPIKE Essential (the smaller set)

SPIKE Essential is for younger students (ages 6–10, Explore division). FLL Challenge requires **SPIKE Prime**. The sets look similar but are different products with different hubs and software.

### Third-party "FLL kits"

Some online sellers offer "FLL kits" that aren't official LEGO Education products. These may use compatible LEGO elements but don't include the SPIKE Prime hub, which is required. Only official SPIKE Prime sets are FLL-legal.

## Getting help with cost

FLL gear is a real expense. Options to reduce it:

**Grants:**
- FIRST offers grant resources at [firstinspires.org/ways-to-help/grants](https://www.firstinspires.org/ways-to-help/grants)
- Many regional affiliates have hardship funds for new teams
- Local community foundations, PTAs, and STEM-focused businesses sometimes sponsor robotics teams

**School support:**
- Some schools purchase SPIKE Prime sets as classroom equipment and let teams use them
- Title I schools in the US may be eligible for LEGO Education grants

**Fundraising:**
- Car washes, bake sales, and crowdfunding (GoFundMe, DonorsChoose for classroom-based teams) are common
- Local businesses often sponsor robotics teams in exchange for a mention in communications

**Shared team costs:**
- Teams typically split registration and Challenge Set costs among families
- Some teams charge a small participation fee to cover consumables and team shirts

## Year 2 and beyond

Once you've committed to FLL for a second season:

- **Keep your SPIKE Prime set** — it's reusable indefinitely. The hub and motors don't expire; only the Challenge Set changes each year.
- **Budget ~$75–$100/year** for the new seasonal Challenge Set.
- **Team registration** renews annually at roughly the same cost.

The big upfront cost is year 1. After that, the annual cost is significantly lower.
