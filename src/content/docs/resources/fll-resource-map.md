A curated map of where to actually go for FIRST LEGO League information. Every link below is annotated with what it is, who it's for, and when to use it. We don't republish content from these sources — we point at them. The official sources are authoritative; everything else is independent and listed with that flag.

## Official sources (canonical)

### FIRST LEGO League — program home

- **URL:** https://www.firstlegoleague.org
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** families, coaches, teachers, teams
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** fll, official, registration
- **Use when:** You need program-wide announcements, registration info, season news, or canonical guidance on how FLL works.

The official FIRST LEGO League program site, run by FIRST. Authoritative source for program structure, season information, registration, and official guidance. Bookmark this — it's the source of truth for anything FLL-program-related.

### FIRST Inspires — parent organization

- **URL:** https://www.firstinspires.org
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** families, coaches, teachers, teams
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** fll, ftc, frc, official, parent-organization
- **Use when:** You're navigating across FIRST programs (FLL → FTC → FRC progression), or need policy/youth-protection information.

The parent organization behind FLL, FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC), and FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC). Useful for understanding the broader pipeline kids progress through, and for official policy documents (Youth Protection Program, mentor agreements, etc.).

### Season Challenge & Robot Game Rule Book

- **URL:** https://www.firstlegoleague.org/season
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, teams, judges, parents
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** fll, rules, season, must-read
- **Use when:** Always — the game manual is the most important document of the season. Read it before designing strategy or building.

Each August FIRST releases the season's Challenge guide and the Robot Game Rule Book. These are the only authoritative source for missions, point values, penalties, and judging rubrics. If anything in our wiki contradicts the current season manual, the manual wins.

### LEGO Education — SPIKE Prime / Mindstorms

- **URL:** https://education.lego.com
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, teachers, families
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** fll, spike-prime, mindstorms, hardware, lesson-plans
- **Use when:** You need official documentation, software downloads, or lesson plans for the FLL hardware kits.

Official LEGO Education portal. Hardware product pages, software downloads (LEGO SPIKE App, LEGO Education App), official lesson plans, and teacher resources. The canonical source for SPIKE Prime and Mindstorms-related questions.

## Event finders

### RobotEvents

- **URL:** https://www.robotevents.com
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** families, coaches, teams
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** fll, vex, events, registration, tournaments
- **Use when:** You're looking for tournaments near you, registering for an event, or checking match schedules and rankings.

The shared event platform used by FIRST and VEX programs. Search by region, register teams, view brackets and rankings. Canonical source for "where's the nearest tournament?"

## Independent resources we trust

### FIRST LEGO League — official YouTube channel

- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/@FIRSTLEGOLeague
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** families, coaches, teachers
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** fll, video, season-reveals
- **Use when:** You want season reveal videos, official program announcements, or sample team spotlights.

Official FLL YouTube. Best place for the August season reveal each year. Not a tutorial channel — coach/team build tutorials live on independent channels.

### FLLCasts

- **URL:** https://www.fllcasts.com
- **Authority:** independent
- **Audience:** kids, coaches
- **Level:** beginner, intermediate
- **Tags:** fll, video, build-tutorials, paid
- **Use when:** A team needs structured, tutorial-style guidance on builds and missions beyond what the official channel provides.

Long-running independent platform with video courses on FLL building and programming. Some content free, deeper courses paid. Used by many coaches as a structured supplement to their own teaching.

## Communities

### Local Facebook groups & coach communities

- **Authority:** community
- **Audience:** coaches, parents
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** fll, community, peer-support
- **Use when:** You want region-specific advice, last-minute season questions, or to connect with other coaches in your area.

Most regions have active Facebook groups for FLL coaches and parents — search "[your state/country] FLL coaches" on Facebook. Quality varies by region, but they're often the fastest path to answers like "where's the nearest mat to borrow before regionals?" Not a primary learning resource, but valuable for peer support.

### r/FIRSTRobotics on Reddit

- **URL:** https://www.reddit.com/r/FIRSTRobotics/
- **Authority:** community
- **Audience:** kids, teens, coaches
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** fll, ftc, frc, community, q-and-a
- **Use when:** You want general Q&A across FIRST programs and a community willing to debate strategy and hardware choices.

Cross-program FIRST community. Skewed toward FTC and FRC but FLL questions get answered. Good for general "is this a normal problem?" sanity checks.
