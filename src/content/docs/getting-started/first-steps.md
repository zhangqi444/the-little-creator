You just said yes to robotics. Maybe your kid begged you. Maybe a teacher signed the whole class up. Either way, you're here, and you're probably wondering where to start.

This guide covers the first week across all six programs. Pick your program and follow the steps — you don't need to read everything.

---

## Quick-start by program

| Program | Ages | First thing to do |
|---|---|---|
| FLL Discover | 4–6 | Find a school or library running a session; no home team needed |
| FLL Explore | 6–10 | Register at [my.firstinspires.org](https://my.firstinspires.org) + your regional PDO |
| FLL Challenge | 9–16 | Register team + order SPIKE Prime kit; see [How to Register](/guides/registration-guide/) |
| VEX IQ (VIQRC) | 8–14 | Register on [RobotEvents.com](https://www.robotevents.com) + order VEX IQ kit |
| FTC | grades 7–12 | Register at [my.firstinspires.org](https://my.firstinspires.org) + order REV Control Hub starter kit |
| FRC | grades 9–12 | Talk to a mentor network; most rookies join an existing team or affiliate with a school |
| VEX V5 (V5RC) | grades 7–12 | Register on [RobotEvents.com](https://www.robotevents.com) + order V5 Classroom Starter Kit |
| VEX U (VURC) | college | Register on [RobotEvents.com](https://www.robotevents.com); recruit two sub-teams of 5+ |

## Step 1 — Register before anything else

**Why first:** Registration windows close. Spots fill. Competition slots are often first-come, first-served at the regional level.

- **FLL (all divisions) and FTC:** National registration at [my.firstinspires.org](https://my.firstinspires.org). Then find your state or country's PDO (Program Delivery Partner) and register regionally — this is how you get assigned to a qualifier. See [How to Register](/guides/registration-guide/).
- **VEX programs (IQ, V5, U):** Team registration through [RobotEvents.com](https://www.robotevents.com). Your team number is assigned here.
- **FRC:** Through [my.firstinspires.org](https://my.firstinspires.org). FRC registration opens in the fall — if you're starting after that, you may be joining an existing team this season and registering your own for next year.

If you're not sure which competition season you're entering, check the current-season page on your program's official site. Seasons typically run August–April (FLL, FTC, FRC) or September–May (VEX).

## Step 2 — Get the hardware

You don't need to be an expert before you buy. Get the recommended kit and open the box together as a team — that's a legitimate first meeting.

| Program | What to order | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| FLL Challenge | SPIKE Prime Core Set (or SPIKE Essential for Explore) | Comes with the Hub, motors, sensors, and enough bricks for most missions |
| VEX IQ | VEX IQ Classroom Starter Kit or Competition Kit | Starter has enough for most builds; Competition adds extra motors |
| FTC | REV Control Hub Starter Kit | Pairs with Android phone or Driver Station; check your regional league's rules |
| FRC | KitBot (shipped to registered teams) | Arrives after Kickoff — don't buy separately |
| VEX V5 | V5 Classroom Starter Kit or V5 Competition Starter Kit | Includes V5 Brain, Smart Motors, Clawbot build |
| VEX U | VURC rules allow any commercial components | Budget for two robots; teams often source hardware from V5 base or custom |

For FLL specifically, see the [FLL Equipment Guide](/guides/equipment-guide/) before ordering — it explains what you actually need vs. nice-to-haves.

**Don't over-buy in week one.** Get the starter kit. Add specialty parts only after you understand the challenge game.

Install the programming environment on at least one laptop before your first meeting. Updates take time and Wi-Fi at practice spaces is unreliable.

## Step 5 — Plan your first meeting

Your first meeting does not need to produce a working robot. It should accomplish exactly three things:

1. **Everyone introduces themselves.** Kids, coaches, parents if present.
2. **Someone reads the scoring rules aloud.** Together, as a group. Or watch the season reveal video.
3. **Someone touches the robot.** Even if it's just unboxing the kit. Hands on hardware = momentum.

That's it. Don't try to programme in week one. Don't try to plan the whole season in week one. Just build trust and excitement.

### What to say to nervous kids

- "You don't need to know anything yet — that's why we're here."
- "Everyone starts not knowing. Everyone figures it out."
- "Your job right now is to be curious."

### What to say to nervous coaches

Same thing.

## What can wait until week 2 and beyond

Things new teams try to do in week one that cause unnecessary stress:

- Building a complete robot (you don't know the challenge well enough yet)
- Writing code (same reason)
- Deciding team roles and hierarchy permanently
- Reading the entire game manual cover to cover (just the scoring rules for now)
- Buying extra parts beyond the starter kit

The [FLL First Four Weeks guide](/guides/first-four-weeks/) has a week-by-week plan for FLL coaches specifically. For FTC, see the [First FTC Tournament guide](/guides/first-ftc-tournament/) for what the season builds toward. For FRC, see the [First FRC Tournament guide](/guides/first-frc-tournament/).

## Common first-week mistakes

| Mistake | What to do instead |
|---|---|
| Waiting until you feel "ready" | Start. Readiness comes from doing. |
| Buying extra parts before understanding the game | Get the starter kit; add parts after week 3 |
| One person driving all the decisions | Let kids (and other adults) own pieces from day one |
| Skipping registration because "the season just started" | Spots fill fast — register within 24 hours of deciding |
| Trying to build a competition-legal robot in week one | Explore the kit freely — no constraints needed yet |
| Not reading the scoring rules before the first meeting | 15 minutes of reading = 60 minutes of saved confusion |

---

## You're not behind

Every team starts from zero. The teams that do well in their first season are not the ones with the most experience — they're the ones who started early, showed up consistently, and let kids lead.

You've got this.
