A practical timeline for one FLL season. Drawn from how seasons actually run, not how they look in a brochure. Adapt to your team's pace — these are reference points, not deadlines.

## Season shape at a glance

FLL runs roughly **August → April** for teams that advance:

- **August** — season reveal; year's challenge dropped
- **September–October** — build, program, research
- **November–December** — qualifying tournaments
- **January–February** — regional / state championships
- **March–April** — World Festival (a small fraction of teams)

Most teams' season ends at qualifiers. That's normal — getting to qualifiers is itself a major accomplishment for a first-year team.

## Pre-season (June–July, before the reveal)

Things you can do *before* the season starts that pay off all year:

- **Confirm the team roster.** Who's in, who's out. Reconfirm; people drift over summer.
- **Set the meeting cadence.** Once a week is the bare minimum; twice a week ramps up nicely closer to competition.
- **Make sure hardware works.** Charge batteries, update SPIKE App, replace lost pieces.
- **Pre-read coach materials.** The previous season's Coach Handbook still applies for everything except season-specific game rules. Skim it.
- **Lock down the meeting space.** A garage, basement, classroom, or library room — somewhere with a table, power, and storage for the kit.
- **Decide on a notebook system.** Engineering notebook can be paper, Google Docs, or markdown — pick one and start the week of kickoff.

## August — Kickoff

**Goal:** Everyone on the team understands the season, no one is panicked.

- Watch the season reveal video together (it's an event — make popcorn).
- Download the **Challenge guide** and **Robot Game Rule Book** from firstlegoleague.org/season. Read the Robot Game rules out loud as a team — slowly. Highlight any rule that affects scoring.
- Look at the mat layout. Identify each mission and its point value. Don't decide which to attempt yet.
- Register your team on FIRST's site (if you haven't).
- Order any missing parts (mat, mission models, replacement gears, charging cables).
- Open a notebook and date the first entry.

**What to skip in August:** Don't start building yet. Spend the first 1–2 weeks understanding the game. Teams that build first and read rules later lose points to penalties.

## September — Build & explore

**Goal:** The team has built a basic driving base and tested it. They've sketched ideas for 2–3 missions.

- **Build the standard driving base** (instructions in the SPIKE App). Don't customize yet.
- **Pick 3–5 missions to attempt** — start with high-value or geometrically simple ones. Don't try all the missions; teams that try everything finish nothing.
- **Sketch attachments on paper before building** them. Saves hours.
- **Start the Innovation Project research.** This runs parallel to the robot work all season — see the [Innovation Project timeline](#innovation-project-timeline-runs-in-parallel) below.
- **Begin engineering notebook discipline:** every session, kids write 2–3 sentences about what they tried and what they learned.

## October — Iterate

**Goal:** Robot reliably completes 2–3 missions in a 2.5-minute match. Innovation Project topic and solution are decided.

- Time your runs. Track scores in a simple spreadsheet — date, mission set, score, notes.
- Eliminate wobbles. A robot that drifts 2 cm per straight-drive run will compound into mission failure by run 5.
- Refine attachments — most attempts will fail twice before working.
- Lock in the Innovation Project solution and start building presentation materials.
- **Schedule a mock judging session** for late October — invite parents or another team's coaches as judges.

## November — Competition ready

**Goal:** The team feels confident in robot, project, and Core Values. Equipment is packed.

- Register for a qualifying tournament (most regions have several to choose from).
- Run **full mock matches** with a timer, including the 30-second pre-match setup.
- Practice presentations in front of strangers. The kids' nervousness on competition day is proportional to how few times they've presented to non-family.
- Pack a **pit kit** (see [Pit kit checklist](#pit-kit-checklist) below).
- Print everything that needs to be on paper — engineering notebook, Innovation Project handouts.
- **Stop introducing major changes 2 weeks out.** Refine only. The teams that change strategy in week-of-tournament regret it.

## Competition day

- Arrive **early** — pit setup and check-in take longer than expected.
- Set up your pit area: kit on the table, chargers plugged in, notebook visible.
- Test the robot once (not 20 times) before queuing for first match.
- Encourage each other — nervousness is normal. So is a robot doing nothing on the first run. So is a robot doing everything on the second.
- Take photos. Watch other teams' robots — strategy ideas for next season come from this more than from coaching.
- After each match, **kids fix and reset the robot themselves.** Coaches advise from outside the pit table per FLL rules.

## After the tournament — reflection

Within a week, run a 60-minute team retro:

- What worked, robot-side?
- What worked, project-side?
- What broke?
- What would we do differently?
- Who wants to come back next year?

Write it down. This is the most valuable input for next season's planning.

## Innovation Project timeline (runs in parallel)

The Innovation Project is judged separately from the Robot Game and is often where rookie teams under-invest. It needs its own timeline:

- **August:** Read the season's Innovation Project prompt. Brainstorm problem areas in your community related to the theme.
- **September:** Pick a topic. Identify a real-world expert to interview (a doctor, engineer, teacher, librarian — someone who knows the problem domain).
- **October (early):** Conduct the expert interview. Write up findings.
- **October (late):** Design your solution — sketches, models, or a digital prototype. It does not have to be physically buildable. Originality matters.
- **November:** Build presentation materials (poster, slides, props). Practice the 5-minute pitch repeatedly.
- **Tournament day:** Present to judges. Take questions. Be honest about what you'd improve.

The Innovation Project is where the "discovery" Core Value really shows. Teams that take it seriously stand out.

## Core Values — judged continuously

FLL Core Values (Discovery, Innovation, Impact, Inclusion, Teamwork, Fun) are scored at the tournament during a separate judging session, but kids should *practice them every meeting*. Specifically:

- **Inclusion:** Every kid gets a chance at every role across the season. Rotate.
- **Teamwork:** When a kid says "I can't," the coach's job is to redirect to a teammate, not to fix it.
- **Discovery:** The notebook is your evidence — moments of "we tried X and learned Y" are exactly what judges want to see.
- **Fun:** If the team isn't enjoying it by week 6, something's wrong with how meetings are structured.

## Pit kit checklist

What to pack in a competition-day pit bag:

- Robot, in a protective case
- Spare battery (charged) and charger
- USB cable for the SPIKE hub
- Laptop or tablet with SPIKE App, fully charged
- Backup: USB stick with current program (in case the laptop dies)
- Engineering notebook (paper or device)
- Innovation Project poster + props + handouts
- Team t-shirts or matching bandanas (optional but kids love it)
- Snacks and water (sugar crashes are real)
- Tape, scissors, sharpie, sticky notes
- A small first aid kit
- A printed pit map (if the venue provides one) and tournament schedule

## Common pitfalls (from experience)

- **Trying too many missions.** Five missions completed cleanly beats ten attempted poorly.
- **Building before reading rules.** Penalties tank your score faster than missions add to it.
- **Coaching the kids' answers.** During judging, judges can tell when kids are reciting an adult's words. Let kids answer, even when they fumble.
- **Saving the engineering notebook for "the end."** It's a journal, not a deliverable. Start week 1.
- **Treating Core Values as theater.** Kids who genuinely enjoy each other do well in Core Values judging. You can't fake it in 10 minutes.
- **Ignoring the Innovation Project until November.** It's half your judging score. Start in September or you'll regret it.
