A curated map of where to go for VEX V5/VRC information. Every link is annotated with what it is, who it's for, and when to use it. We don't republish content — we point at it. Official sources are authoritative; everything else is listed as independent or community.

## Official sources (canonical)

### VEX V5 — hardware platform page

- **URL:** https://www.vex.com/vex-v5
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, teams, families, teachers
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** vex-v5, official, hardware, v5-brain, smart-motors
- **Use when:** You need an overview of the VEX V5 system — what the V5 Brain and Smart Motors are, how the V5 ecosystem fits into the broader VEX lineup, and whether V5 is the right step up from VEX IQ.

The main VEX V5 landing page. Covers the V5 Brain (full-color touchscreen, built-in wi-fi), V5 Smart Motors with integrated encoders, and the sensor/controller ecosystem. Good first stop for families deciding between VEX IQ and V5, or coaches comparing V5 to FTC hardware.

### VEX Robotics Competition (V5/VRC) — current game page

- **URL:** https://www.vexrobotics.com/v5-current-game
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, teams (grades 7–12), families
- **Level:** intermediate, advanced
- **Tags:** vex-v5, vrc, competition, season, current-game, game-manual
- **Use when:** You need the current VRC season's game information — field setup, scoring, game manual, and official season materials. Read the game manual before designing robot strategy or scouting opponents.

The official V5RC current game page. Each season introduces a new game (typically announced in late April at the World Championship). This page links to the game manual, field specifications, scoring rules, and official season materials. **The game manual is the authoritative source for competition rules** — always check the current manual when rules questions arise, as our guides may not reflect the latest season.

The official Robotics Education & Competition Foundation (RECF) knowledge base for the VEX Robotics Competition (V5RC). ### RobotEvents — event finder and team registration

- **URL:** https://www.robotevents.com
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, teams, families
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** vex-v5, vrc, events, registration, team-finder, skills-challenge
- **Use when:** Finding local VRC tournaments, registering your team for events, checking Robot Skills Challenge standings, scouting opponent teams, or looking up team rosters in your region.

The official RECF event management platform. For VRC teams: select program "VEX Robotics Competition" (V5RC), then filter by date, location, and season. Robot Skills Challenge rankings — which can qualify a team for the World Championship as Skills-only — are tracked here. Creating a free account is required for registration; team members can also access event schedules, match results, and OPR statistics via third-party apps that pull from RobotEvents.

### VEXcode Pro V5 — official programming environment

- **URL:** https://www.vex.com/vexcode
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** teams, coaches
- **Level:** beginner, intermediate
- **Tags:** vex-v5, vexcode, blocks, c-plus-plus, python, programming, download
- **Use when:** Downloading the official VEX V5 programming environment; starting with blocks before moving to text-based code; or using the built-in Python API docs.

The free official programming environment for VEX V5. Supports three modes: **Blocks** (drag-and-drop, beginner-friendly), **Python** (natural transition for SPIKE Prime / VEX IQ Python alumni), and **C++ (Text)** (same environment as PROS but without the full PROS ecosystem). Available for Windows, macOS, and Linux. Rookie teams typically start with Blocks or Python before graduating to C++.

## REC Foundation resources

VEX hardware is made by VEX Robotics; VEX *competitions* are run by the **Robotics Education & Competition Foundation (RECF)**, a separate non-profit. Many first-year VRC families miss the RECF side entirely — and miss out on grants, mentor training, and rules Q&A resources.

### REC Foundation — program operator

- **URL:** https://recf.org
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** families, coaches, teachers, event partners
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** recf, vex-v5, vrc, governance, official, grants
- **Use when:** Understanding who runs VRC, finding cross-program policies, or accessing resources like grants and mentor training.

RECF runs all VEX competition programs — V5RC (V5), VIQRC (IQ), VURC (University), VAIRC (AI). RECF handles team registration policies, event partner accreditation, rules processes, mentor training, and the World Championship. If you're coming from FRC: think of RECF as the equivalent of FIRST HQ.

### RECF Trainings — coach and event partner courses

- **URL:** https://recf.org/recf-trainings/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, teachers, event partners
- **Level:** beginner, intermediate
- **Tags:** recf, coach-training, professional-development
- **Use when:** You're a new VRC coach who wants structured RECF training on running a team, judging a design interview, or volunteering at events.

Free RECF training tracks for adults supporting VEX teams. The "new coach" track explains how the season and event structure works from the inside — useful even if you'll never be an event partner. Event partner training is required if you want to host a tournament.

### Team Grants — RECF

- **URL:** https://recf.org/teams/for-participants/grants/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** new coaches, teachers, parents starting a team
- **Level:** beginner
- **Tags:** recf, grants, funding, equipment, vex-v5
- **Use when:** You're starting a new VRC team and need help with the hardware investment (~$500–$1,500 for a starter V5 kit).

RECF runs a Team Grant Program matching schools and community organisations with sponsor-funded resources — typically a free or discounted starter kit. Eligibility varies by sponsor (some grants are region-specific; some target Title I schools or underserved students). Apply early — popular grants close quickly. See also our [Team Funding guide](/guides/team-funding/) for broader fundraising strategies.

### Girl Powered — RECF initiative

- **URL:** https://recf.org/teams/for-participants/girl-powered/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** coaches, parents, all-girls and co-ed teams
- **Level:** all
- **Tags:** recf, girl-powered, diversity, inclusion
- **Use when:** You coach or want to recruit girls into VRC, or you want to host a Girl Powered outreach workshop.

RECF's Girl Powered initiative (presented by Google) funds workshops, awards, and outreach materials for getting and keeping more girls in robotics. At VRC events, many regions offer a separate Girl Powered Award. Includes a workshop facilitation kit. Inclusion principles from the program apply to co-ed teams as well.

## Season & events

### VEX Robotics World Championship

- **URL:** https://recf.org/vex-robotics-world-championship/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** qualifying teams, coaches, families planning long-term
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** vex-v5, vrc, worlds, championship, qualification
- **Use when:** Your team is approaching qualification, you want to understand the top of VRC, or you're watching live streams to motivate your team.

The annual culmination of all VEX programs, held in late April (2026 edition: St. Louis, April 21–30). VRC teams qualify through regional and state events, or via Robot Skills Challenge standings. Check the [V5RC KB](https://v5rc-kb.recf.org/hc/en-us) for your region's specific qualification pathway. Watching World Championship matches on the live stream is one of the best things you can do for team motivation.

### RECF Signature Events

- **URL:** https://recf.org/teams/competition/recf-signature-events/
- **Authority:** official
- **Audience:** competitive VRC teams, coaches
- **Level:** intermediate, advanced
- **Tags:** recf, vrc, signature-events, regional
- **Use when:** Your team is ready for a step up from local qualifiers — Signature Events draw multi-region teams and serve as a major proving ground before the World Championship.

RECF-designated regional tournaments larger than typical local events (often 80–100+ teams). Design interview depth, competition intensity, and scouting opportunities are noticeably higher than at a local qualifier. Attending a Signature Event mid-season is a good benchmark for a team aiming for Worlds.

## Programming resources

### PROS — open-source C++ framework for VEX V5

- **URL:** https://pros.cs.purdue.edu
- **Authority:** independent
- **Audience:** teams (advanced programmers), teens with C++ experience
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** vex-v5, pros, c-plus-plus, autonomous, programming, open-source
- **Use when:** Your team has C++ experience and wants more control over autonomous routines than VEXcode provides — custom schedulers, RTOS tasks, or integration with autonomous motion libraries like LemLib.

PROS (Purdue Robotics Operating System) is the open-source alternative to VEXcode for VRC. Written in C++, it runs on the V5 Brain and offers RTOS-level task scheduling, better debugging output, and integration with third-party libraries. Most competitive VRC teams above regional level use PROS + LemLib or similar. Not recommended for rookies — start with VEXcode; migrate when you outgrow it.

### LemLib — autonomous movement library for PROS

- **URL:** https://lemlib.readthedocs.io
- **Authority:** community
- **Audience:** teams with PROS experience
- **Level:** advanced
- **Tags:** vex-v5, pros, lemlib, autonomous, odometry, pid, motion
- **Use when:** You're using PROS and want a production-ready autonomous framework with odometry, PID heading correction, and pure-pursuit path following.

LemLib is the most widely adopted autonomous movement library for VRC (PROS-based). Provides wheel-odometry pose tracking, motion profiling, and heading correction. Used by many teams at the World Championship level. Requires PROS familiarity and at least one season of robot programming experience. The documentation includes worked examples and tuning guides. See our [VEX V5 Programming Basics](/guides/vex-v5-programming-basics/) guide for prerequisites.
