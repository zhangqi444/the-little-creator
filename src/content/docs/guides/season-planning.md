A rough timeline and checklist to keep your team on track from kickoff to competition day.

## FLL Season Timeline

### August — Kickoff
- Watch the season reveal (it's an event — make it fun!)
- Download and read the game manual carefully
- Register your team on the FIRST website
- Confirm team roster and meeting schedule
- Order any missing hardware/parts

### September — Build & Explore
- Study the game board missions — understand scoring before building
- Start building and testing robot attachments
- Begin brainstorming the Innovation Project topic
- Establish a code repository (GitHub is great for this)

### October — Iterate
- Test and refine robot programs
- Score your runs and track improvement
- Finalize Innovation Project research and solution
- Practice presentations and judging sessions

### November — Competition Ready
- Register for qualifying tournament
- Do full mock competitions (timed runs, presentations, judges Q&A)
- Prepare your pit setup and equipment checklist
- Rest — seriously, burnout is real

### Competition Day
- Arrive early, set up your pit area
- Warm up robot, verify all programs load correctly
- Encourage each other — nervousness is normal
- Have fun and take notes on what to improve next season

## General Tips

**Track your runs.** Keep a simple log of each practice run: date, score, what worked, what didn't. This data is invaluable when making decisions under time pressure.

**Protect the last two weeks.** Don't introduce major changes in the final two weeks before competition. Refinement only.

**Sleep and food matter.** This applies to kids and coaches both.
