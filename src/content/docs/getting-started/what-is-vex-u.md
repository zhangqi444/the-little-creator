VEX U (formally the **VEX U Robotics Competition**, or **VURC**) is the university-level VEX competition. It runs in parallel to the high-school program (V5RC / VRC) using the same V5 hardware platform, but with substantially loosened build restrictions and a few rule modifications that let college teams pursue more ambitious mechanical and software designs.

VEX U currently has around 200 active teams worldwide. It's small relative to V5RC (which has thousands), but it's the natural continuation pathway for V5 alumni heading into engineering programs — and it's the on-ramp for university teams that want competitive robotics without committing to the scale and cost of FRC.

:::note[Who runs VEX U?]
VEX U, like all VEX competitions, is operated by the [Robotics Education & Competition Foundation (RECF)](/resources/vex-resource-map/#rec-foundation--program-operator) — separate from VEX Robotics, the company that makes the hardware. See the VEX Resource Map for the [VURC program entry](/resources/vex-resource-map/#vex-u-robotics-competition-vurc) and the [REC Library](/resources/vex-resource-map/#rec-library--per-program-knowledge-bases-pattern) where the official VURC knowledge base lives.
:::

## Who is VEX U for?

- **Currently-enrolled university students** — undergraduate, graduate, or community college. Eligibility is checked at registration; this is the one constraint that's hard to work around.
- **A faculty or staff mentor** is required, similar to other VEX programs.
- **Team size is flexible.** Practical teams run 5–10 active students; some schools field two or three VEX U teams from the same robotics club.

Note: There's no dual-enrollment shortcut. A high school student taking a college class isn't VEX U-eligible — they should compete in V5RC instead.

## How VEX U differs from V5RC

The two programs play the same seasonal game (e.g. *Push Back* for 2025-26, *Override* for 2026-27) on the same 12'×12' field. The differences are in what teams are *allowed* to build:

| Restriction | V5RC | VEX U |
|---|---|---|
| Robot size | 18"×18"×18" starting, expansion limits | Typically more permissive starting envelope, with allowance for two cooperative robots per alliance |
| Custom mechanical parts | Mostly stock VEX V5 parts; limited custom polycarbonate / 3D-printed pieces | Significant custom-machined parts allowed (aluminum, polycarbonate, 3D-printed structural components) |
| Custom electronics | Stock V5 motors and sensors only | Custom sensors and limited custom electronics permitted in specified categories |
| Robots per alliance | Two robots, one team per robot | Two robots per team — teams build both |

The practical effect: VEX U teams have room for ambitious mechanical and software design choices that would be illegal at the high-school level. Many V5 alumni cite this — the freedom to actually engineer the robot rather than work around stock-part constraints — as the appeal.

Game rules themselves are nearly identical between V5RC and VURC, with VEX U-specific modifications spelled out in the **VURC game manual addendum** published alongside the main game manual each season.

## The transition: when does VEX U make sense?

Most VEX U teams form one of three ways:

1. **V5RC alumni continuing into college.** A student who competed in V5 in high school enrols at a university, finds (or starts) a VEX U team, and brings their playbook. This is by far the most common pathway.
2. **New university robotics programs.** A school that doesn't have a tradition of FRC or a robotics major often picks VEX U as a cost-effective starting point — entry cost is dramatically lower than FRC, infrastructure is simpler, and the seasonal cycle is more forgiving for a club that meets a few hours a week.
3. **Engineering project courses.** Some universities run VEX U participation as part of a capstone or junior design project, with the competition serving as the structured deliverable.

It is *not* a fit if a college student is already deeply invested in FRC mentorship (those overlap in time-of-year and energy) or if the goal is professional-tier robot fabrication — VEX U's hardware envelope is intentionally bounded by the V5 platform.

## First steps for a new VEX U team

1. **Confirm eligibility.** All competing students must be enrolled at the listed university.
2. **Register a team.** Through [RobotEvents.com](/resources/vex-resource-map/#robotevents--event-finder-and-team-registration), under the VEX U Robotics Competition program. Each university can register multiple teams.
3. **Read the VURC game manual.** Published each year alongside the V5RC manual; covers both shared rules and VEX U-specific addenda. Linked from the [VURC program page](https://recf.org/teams/competition/vex-u-robotics-competition/).
4. **Use the [VURC knowledge base](https://vurc-kb.recf.org/hc/en-us)** for the canonical registration and getting-help articles — RECF's official Q&A for VEX U specifically. Better source than forum threads for "is this rule actually X" questions.
5. **Plan for two robots.** Each VEX U team builds and operates two robots that compete as an alliance. Budget for hardware (two V5 brain sets, batteries, motors), build time, and storage accordingly.
6. **Consider scholarships.** VEX U students remain eligible for the [RECF scholarship directory](/resources/vex-resource-map/#scholarships--recf-partner-universities) (>$110M across 100+ universities). Graduate students aren't always eligible — check the specific scholarship terms.

## Where VEX U fits in the broader landscape

- **vs. FRC:** FRC is the largest collegiate-adjacent FIRST program but operates at university *outreach* level — college students typically mentor a high-school FRC team rather than compete themselves. VEX U is the inverse — college students compete directly. The two coexist on many campuses.
- **vs. VEX AI (VAIRC):** [VAIRC](/resources/vex-resource-map/#vex-ai-robotics-competition-vairc) is a separate RECF program where robots are autonomous-only (no driver) and require computer vision / ML for game-element identification. Open to both high school and university teams. Significantly more software-heavy than VEX U; a smaller community but rapidly growing. Some universities field both a VURC and a VAIRC team.
- **vs. NRC, RobotX, Maritime competitions:** Specialized adult/university competitions exist outside the VEX ecosystem (NASA Robotic Mining Competition, AUVSI RobotX). These tend to be one-off seasonal projects rather than continuing programs.
