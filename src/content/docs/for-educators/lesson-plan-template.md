A single-session lesson plan template. Copy the structure, fill in the placeholders, customize for your group. Designed for sessions of 60–120 minutes — adjust timing for shorter or longer.

## How to use this template

1. Copy the **Template** block below into a new document (or new markdown file in `for-educators/lessons/` if you want to share it back).
2. Fill in the placeholders.
3. Run the lesson. Take notes in the **After-session reflection** block.
4. Update the plan based on what actually happened — that's the most valuable artifact, not the original plan.

## Session timeline

| Time | Activity | Who leads | Notes |
|------|----------|-----------|-------|
| 0:00 — 0:10 | Arrival, recap last session | Coach | Quick win: kids share one thing from last week |
| 0:10 — 0:25 | Direct instruction: <topic> | Coach | Demo on screen or one robot |
| 0:25 — 1:10 | Hands-on: <activity> | Kids in pairs | Coaches circulate, ask questions, don't fix |
| 1:10 — 1:25 | Share-out: each pair shows what worked | Kids | One sentence per pair |
| 1:25 — 1:30 | Cleanup, preview next session | Coach | Assign anything for next time |

## Tips from teachers who've done this

- **The timeline always slips.** Plan 90 minutes of activities for a 60-minute session and assume you'll cut. Arrival/cleanup eats more time than you think.
- **Don't fix kids' robots.** When a kid says "it's broken," ask "what do you think is happening?" before troubleshooting. Teach the diagnosis, not the fix.
- **One learning objective per session.** Kids can't actually internalize three. Pick the one and let everything else be supporting.
- **The reflection block is the most valuable part.** A binder of post-session reflections is worth more than a binder of pristine lesson plans for the next teacher.
