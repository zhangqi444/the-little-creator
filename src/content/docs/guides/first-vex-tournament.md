This guide is the VEX counterpart to [Your First FLL Tournament](/guides/first-tournament-expectations/). The emotional advice transfers — first tournaments feel chaotic, telling your team that ahead of time helps — but VEX tournaments have a distinct structure shared across VEX IQ (VIQRC), VEX V5 (V5RC), and VEX U (VURC). This page covers the common pattern with program-specific differences flagged.

:::note[Three programs, one structure]
VEX IQ, V5RC, and VEX U all use the same tournament structure: qualifying matches → Skills Challenge → alliance selection → elimination matches → awards. The differences are in robot size, team composition, and game mechanics. Specific differences are noted throughout.
:::

## How VEX tournaments are structured

A standard VEX tournament — a **regional qualifier** at the most common scale — runs as a full day, typically 8am to 6pm. The structure is consistent across IQ, V5, and U:

| Time (approx.) | What happens |
|---|---|
| 7:30–9:00 am | Check-in, pit setup, inspection |
| 9:00 am | Driver's meeting |
| 9:00 am – 12:30 pm | Qualifying matches (round 1) + Skills Challenge runs |
| 12:30 pm | Lunch |
| 1:00 – 3:30 pm | Qualifying matches (round 2) + Skills Challenge final attempts |
| 3:30 pm | Alliance selection |
| 4:00 – 5:30 pm | Elimination matches |
| 5:30 – 6:00 pm | Awards ceremony |

Larger events (state championships, VEX Worlds qualifiers) run longer; some span 2–3 days. The single-day format above is what your first tournament most likely looks like.

## The Skills Challenge — VEX's unique element

Unlike FLL, FTC, or FRC, VEX has a parallel **Skills Challenge** running throughout the day in addition to the alliance matches. A single robot runs the field alone in two formats:

- **Driver Skills** (60 seconds) — driver controls the robot trying to score as much as possible
- **Programming Skills** (60 seconds) — robot runs entirely autonomously

Each team gets multiple attempts; only the best Driver + best Programming score combine for the team's Skills Challenge ranking. Skills standings are separate from match standings and have their own awards. **Skills Challenge often determines World Championship qualification independent of alliance match performance** — invest in it accordingly.

## The four things that will surprise a new VEX team

1. **Two simultaneous competitions are running.** Alliance matches on the main field, Skills Challenge on a side field, often at the same time. Send students to *both*. Don't lose Skills opportunities while you're focused on alliance matches.
2. **Alliance selection works differently than FRC.** The top *qualifying* teams pick partners, but the picked teams *can decline* and instead form their own alliance with another team. This is more strategic and more emotional than the FRC version.
3. **Pit life is intense and friendly.** You'll work in your pit between matches, but you'll also be visited by other teams' scouts, judges (for Excellence Award judging), and curious families. Have a presentable pit with your team identity on display.
4. **VEX U-specific: two robots per team.** Unlike VEX IQ and V5RC, VEX U teams operate *two* robots simultaneously per alliance — both built by the same team. This doubles your inspection burden, your maintenance burden, and your driver coordination burden.

## Inspection in VEX

VEX inspection checks:

- **Size** — fits in the program's specified sizing box (varies by program; check the season's manual)
- **Weight** (V5/V5RC only — IQ and U have different constraints)
- **Components** — all parts on the robot are from the legal parts list (VEX IQ is strictest about this; V5 allows some custom; VEX U allows the most customisation)
- **License plates** — team number visible on three sides
- **Software** — robot connects to the field control system, autonomous selector works
- **Field elements** — robot doesn't damage field elements when interacting

Inspectors are helpful and want you on the field. Bring a small toolkit: hex keys, screwdriver, electrical tape, spare zip ties. Fix what they catch; re-inspect.

## How VEX program differences affect your day

### VEX IQ (VIQRC)

- Smaller pit footprint (single 8'×8' or 10'×10')
- Robots fit in an 11"×19"×15" sizing box
- Match teams play *two robots at a time* in a 2-team alliance, but team rosters are smaller (often 2–3 students per robot)
- Family-heavy event culture — parents and siblings often present and involved

### VEX V5 (V5RC)

- Standard 10'×10' pit
- Robots fit in an 18"×18"×18" expansion box
- Faster pace; teen-heavy culture
- More aggressive defensive play in matches; pit repairs more common

### VEX U (VURC)

- Two robots per team in every match — you're effectively running two simultaneous programs
- Roughly 200 teams worldwide, so you'll likely recognise other competitors
- Adult culture — more professional vibe than V5RC, but less than FRC
- See [Starting a VEX U Team](/guides/vex-u-first-team/) for the broader VEX U context

## Awards: what gets handed out

VEX awards (consistent across IQ, V5, U with program-specific naming variations):

- **Excellence Award** — top judged award, recognises the team strongest across all aspects of the program (robot, judging, Skills, GP). Often the qualification path to the next event level. See [VEX Awards](/guides/vex-awards/) for the full breakdown.
- **Tournament Champions** — winning alliance from elimination
- **Tournament Finalists** — losing alliance from elimination
- **Robot Skills Champion** — top combined Skills score
- **Design Award** — recognises strong engineering process via notebook and interview
- **Build Award** — recognises mechanical execution
- **Amaze Award** — recognises a "wow factor" performance or design

## How to help your team navigate the day

**Before:**

- Walk through the schedule together the night before; know your first match and your first Skills slot.
- Pack two charged batteries and bring chargers (Skills runs deplete batteries fast).
- Print your engineering notebook for judging.

**During qualification + Skills:**

- Send one student to each Skills attempt; rotate so multiple students get experience.
- Between matches, debrief briefly. Note what worked, what to change.
- Scout your alliance-selection-relevant opponents — what's their autonomous? Best teleop strategy?

**During Excellence / Design judging:**

- A judging team visits each pit during the day (often twice — once early, once during Skills). Have at least two students ready to walk a judge through your robot in 3 minutes.
- Bring the engineering notebook to the pit interview; reference it specifically when answering questions about your design process.

**At alliance selection:**

- Before the ceremony, decide as a team: what's our pick order? Have a ranked list of teams you'd want.
- If you're getting picked: usually accept the first offer. Declining is risky.
- If you're not picked: acknowledge it out loud ("we'll come back stronger") — public composure helps your team move past it.

**Awards ceremony:**

- Sit together, cheer for other teams winning, embody Gracious Professionalism.
- Debrief that night or the next day. What surprised us? What changes before the next event?

## VEX-specific additions to the packing list

On top of the general [Tournament Day Checklist](/guides/tournament-day-checklist/):

- **Two charged batteries minimum** (Skills runs hit batteries hard)
- **Engineering notebook** (judges expect to see it during pit interview)
- **Spare wheels and a few common parts** (mecanum / omni wheels can break)
- **Electrical tape, zip ties, hex keys, screwdriver** (basic pit repair kit)
- **VEX team pit banner** if you have one — pit identity matters for Excellence judging

## Where this differs from FLL Challenge

| Aspect | FLL Challenge | VEX (any program) |
|---|---|---|
| Match format | One team per run | Two-team alliances |
| Skills component | None | Separate Skills Challenge (often advancement-relevant) |
| Judging style | Three sessions | Pit interview during the day + notebook review |
| Alliance selection | None | Yes — captains pick + picked teams can decline |
| Tournament length | Half-day | Full day |
