A section for the people running robotics in classrooms, after-school clubs, libraries, and community programs. Most of the rest of this wiki is written for families and students. This section assumes you're managing a group — possibly with a budget, a schedule, and learning objectives to hit.

Covers all six youth competitive robotics programs: **FLL** (ages 4–16), **FTC** (grades 7–12), **FRC** (grades 9–12), **VEX IQ** (ages 8–14), **VEX V5/VRC** (grades 8–12), and **VEX U** (college).

## Curriculum starters

Eight-week onboarding outlines — one per program. Start with the one that matches your kit and age group; adapt timing and depth to your context.

- **[FLL Curriculum Starter](/for-educators/curriculum-starter/)** — LEGO Education SPIKE Prime, FLL Challenge format, ages 9–16.
- **[FTC Curriculum Starter](/for-educators/ftc-curriculum-starter/)** — REV Robotics / goBILDA hardware, FTC SDK Java, grades 7–12.
- **[FRC Curriculum Starter](/for-educators/frc-curriculum-starter/)** — roboRIO + WPILib Java, FRC competition format, grades 9–12.
- **[VEX IQ Curriculum Starter](/for-educators/vex-iq-curriculum-starter/)** — VEX IQ snap-together kit, VEXcode IQ Blocks / Python, ages 8–14.
- **[VEX V5/VRC Curriculum Starter](/for-educators/vex-v5-curriculum-starter/)** — VEX V5 Brain + Smart Motors, VEXcode Pro V5 C++, grades 8–12.
- **[VEX U / VURC Curriculum Starter](/for-educators/vex-u-curriculum-starter/)** — V5 hardware, PROS C++, engineering notebook depth, college level.

## Who this section is for

- **Classroom teachers** integrating robotics into STEM, technology, or engineering classes
- **After-school club leaders** running an FLL, FTC, FRC, or VEX team across a semester
- **Library and community-program instructors** running short workshops or summer camps
- **Coaches** who happen to also teach the team in a structured way (most of you)

## What this section is *not*

- Not a replacement for the official FIRST or VEX/RECF coach materials. Those remain authoritative for program rules, judging, and registration. See the resource maps for canonical links: [FLL](/resources/fll-resource-map/), [FTC](/resources/ftc-resource-map/), [FRC](/resources/frc-resource-map/), [VEX IQ](/resources/vex-iq-resource-map/), [VEX V5](/resources/vex-v5-resource-map/), [VEX U](/resources/vex-u-resource-map/).
- Not a packaged commercial curriculum. This is community-shared, opinionated guidance from coaches and teachers who've done it before.

## Tone & assumptions

We assume you're not an engineer, and that you may be teaching something you're learning yourself. That's normal in robotics education. The materials here favor honesty about what works, not theoretical pedagogy. Technical depth scales with the program: FLL materials are beginner-friendly; FTC/FRC/VEX V5/VEX U materials assume older students and more complex hardware. If something here doesn't fit your context, ignore it — these are starting points, not prescriptions.

## Contributing

If you're a teacher with a lesson, week-by-week plan, or assessment rubric that worked, please share it. Open a pull request following the same metadata patterns used elsewhere in the wiki. Lessons that include "what went wrong the first time" are especially valued — they save the next teacher months of trial and error.
