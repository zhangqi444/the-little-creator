VEX is a competitive robotics program for students from middle school through university. It offers more engineering complexity than FLL and is a popular stepping stone toward high school robotics and STEM careers.

## VEX Programs

| Program | Age | Hardware |
|---|---|---|
| VEX IQ | 8–14 | Snap-together plastic (similar to LEGO) |
| VEX V5 | 11–18 | Metal components, more advanced |
| VEX U | University | Full custom builds |

For most families coming from FLL, **VEX IQ** is the natural next step. **VEX V5** (also called VRC — VEX Robotics Competition) is what most high school teams compete in. For more on the university-level program, see [What is VEX U?](/getting-started/what-is-vex-u/).

## How VEX Competitions Work

Each season introduces a new game played on a 12×12 ft field. Two alliances of two robots each compete head-to-head in matches. Scoring depends on the game's objectives (stacking, launching, hanging, etc.).

Competitions also include a **Skills Challenge** where a single robot runs the field alone — great practice and a separate ranking.

## Programming in VEX

VEX supports multiple programming options:

- **VEXcode Blocks** — Drag-and-drop, great for beginners
- **VEXcode Python** — Natural progression from blocks
- **VEXcode C++** — Used by more advanced/competitive teams
- **PROS** — Open-source C++ framework, used by top teams

Most kids start with Blocks or Python. C++ becomes relevant when optimizing for championships.

## Key Differences from FLL

- No research project component — it's purely robot competition
- More mechanical engineering involved (building with metal, gears, pneumatics in V5)
- Longer season with more tournaments available
- Stronger pathway to high school and college robotics
